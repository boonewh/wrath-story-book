For several weeks, excitement has been building in Kenabres—Armasse is coming! Traditionally an opportunity for scholars and priests to come together to study the lessons of history from wars past, since Aroden’s death, this holy day has become more about training commoners in weaponry, choosing squires, and ordaining new priests. Over time, Armasse has grown to encompass jousting competitions, mock duels, battle reenactments, and other festival events. Due to the nature of the celebrations, you and Thane have been busy at the forge with orders from many wishing to participate in the competitions. In Kenabres, the festival (which takes place on 16 Arodus) is eagerly anticipated, for it provides distractions from the horrors of being on the front line of the war. Smiles on faces normally marred by downcast eyes and furrowed brows do wonders for city morale in the weeks leading up to the event. Armasse is a citywide celebration, but the majority of the event, including its jousting matches and other entertainments, takes place at Clydwell Plaza, just west of the cathedral. It is a welcome break when you two finally leave the forge to go to and watch the festival.  You have been lucky enough to get a good spot, in attendance near the cathedral’s façade, to observe the opening ceremonies at noon.
Armasse officially began at noon, with the blessing of the festival by
Lord Hulrun himself, ruler of Kenabres. The crowd gathered in Clydwell
Plaza quieted as the aged inquisitor took the stage, clad in shining,
resplendent armor. He cleared his throat, but just as he was about to
speak, a bright light shone from the west, as if the sun were rising
from the wrong direction. Hulrun’s shadow fell huge and distorted across
the cathedral’s facade. A moment later, the sound of a thunderous
explosion ripped through the air and earth, along with a violent tremor.
To the west, the fortress known as the Kite—the location of Kenabres’s
wardstone—had vanished. In its place, a brilliant plume of red fire,
lightning, and smoke erupted into the heavens. A moment later, a
powerful roar accompanied a welcome sight rising from the
crowd—Kenabres’s greatest guardian, the ancient silver dragon
Terendelev, who had until that moment been attending the opening
ceremony disguised as a human. Above, another form appeared, as
nightmarish as the dragon was breathtaking. A humanoid shape three times
the size of any man, with skin coated in fire and lightning, gripped a
flaming sword and whip. The creature’s identity was immediately obvious:
Khorramzadeh, the Storm King of the Worldwound, had come to Kenabres!
As the ground continued to shake and disgorge demons into the streets,
the dragon and the balor lord clashed above. The fight was over in a few
harrowing moments, as the balor cut deep into Terendelev’s body,
swooping down to strike the dragon and arresting her charge. A few more
blows, and the titanic duo spiraled downward toward the crowd.
The sight of the dragon smashing into the facade of the Cathedral of St.
Clydwell is one no witness would ever forget. At that moment, a titanic
demon erupted at the far end of the plaza, reducing several buildings to
ruins as it smashed into this world. The rift it created shot across the
plaza, and this time there was no escape—it opened below your feet,
angling away into darkness. Even as you fell, the dragon noticed your
plight. Though she saw death standing over her, she seized this final
chance to save a few more souls. After she uttered a few arcane words
and stretched out a bleeding talon, you felt her magic take hold of you,
slowing your plummet into the darkness as if you were feathers falling
into a pit. Yet the fall remained as inexorable, and as you drifted
downward into the depths, the last thing you saw was the Storm King
standing before the ancient silver dragon, his sword lashing out and
cleaving full through her neck. As her severed head fell, the rift above
you slammed shut, and the light of the world above was gone.

The people who had been standing in the sunshine wake up in total darkness. A light springs into existence in one area as someone casts a light spell revealing the bodies of several who did not survive the fall. A dwarf stands up and moves to some rubble and starts moving it, trying to free another dwarf. A human woman calls out a name “Thane…” she is trapped as well. Two other people, a half-elf from the look of it and a Aassimar move to help free the trapped dwarf. When the second dwarf is freed, he stands up and moves to the woman who called out. “Anevia” Thane called out. “Be still,” he said as he looked “It looks like  your leg is broken.” The half-elf, named Caleth, was bending over a human male. “Aravashniel, are you hurt.” The man turned to Caleth and Caleth recoiled before he could stop himself. Aravashniel’s face was burned and gashed, his eyes a wreck. He was obviously blind. “Is there a healer? We need a healer!” The dwarf who freed the other dwarf walked up. “Aye, I’m Korroc, what do you need?” Looking over Aravashniel, Korroc shook his head. I can’t heal this, it need more than what I have. The Aassimar that helped earlier nodded to everyone and introduced himself as Nageru.” One other human man stood up and joined the group, everyone immediately realized it was one of the area’s nobles, named Horgus. 
Looking around, eyes adjusting, they started taking stock of where they were. A large, dark cavern. Suddenly, looking around, they were standing near a pile of rubble to one side of the cavern. Quickly they realized there was movement near the middle of the cavern and a large spider came over and crouched. Nageru called out “Everyone watch it, we are not alone.” Caleth immediately moved in front of the injured and Korroc hefted his shield and hammer and said “If its alive, I’ll squish it.” And started moving toward it. Thane and Nageru started forward and then Caleth followed. It didn’t take long for them to realize that the spider was dead, probably for several days. It smelled. And suddenly there was movement inside and there was a squishing and crunch sound from the middle of the spider. Korroc moved up and began casting a spell when suddenly the abdomen burst open and a giant maggot rolled out. Thane moved up and slashed at the maggot and a dagger flew threw the air at the maggot from Caleth. The maggot was dead. A second maggot burst out and bit Thane. Abandoning his spell, Korroc moved around Thane and brought a hammer down heavy on the new maggot. Nageru threw a javelin and skewered the second maggot. Looking around, Nageru realized that he could see silver scales. Sadly, he moved to them, realizing that they were scales from the fallen dragon. As he picked one up, he had a feeling of empathy from the scale and a sense of determination that was transferred to him momentarily. Korroc, Thane and Caleth noticed him standing and holding the scale. They each moved to him and realized that there were more scales. Each picked up one and got the same feeling from it. Gathering up and getting the injured, they started heading in the one direction that was available to them, hoping to find a way out. 
They came to a narrow point where only one of them could squeeze through at a time. Nageru pushed through first and found a stone ledge jutting out from the wall. The floor was about fifteen feet down. They could see a backpack hanging below, a campsite down on the floor and above was a fissure leading up in darkness. Retrieving the backpack revealed supplies that were probably carried by a forgotten crusader at some point. 
Nageru climbed down the short drop into the room. As soon as he was down, he heard noise coming from nearby rubble. Two giant cockroaches scurried out, moving directly to Nageru, who promptly brought a fist down on one, squashing it immediately. Korroc grabbed his newly found scale and tapped into its innate powers, floating down to the floor and moving to the other cockroach. Caleth tried to fire an arrow at the remaining cockroach, but the arrow deflected off Korroc’s armor instead. Korroc didn’t have time to even notice as the cockroach jumped at him. Korroc’s shield deflected the attack. Thane shot an arrow and put a skewer in the cockroach, but it kept moving. Nageru finished the job with a hard kick down on the arrow, driving it through. 
They managed to get everyone down from the ledge and started moving through the camp, searching, but didn’t find much, but they did find a brooch that looked interesting. Horgus looked at it with a practiced eye “About 200 gold in value, but this is a unique piece, it had to have belonged to someone or had meaning.” He shrugged. 
They moved forward and came to another area with a  ledge, but this time they needed to move everyone up. The ledge had an old, but strong looking rope already tied. Nageru went to the ledge and motioned to Korroc. “We are the two strongest. Let me help you up and we can get everyone else up.” Korroc moved to him and took a hoist up and climbed the rope to the top. At that point, they managed to get everyone up with Nageru at the bottom helping push everyone up and Korroc hoisting them over at the top. Moving through narrow passages, and coming to an opening, they realize they are at a cave that looks unsafe. The floors are covered with rubble and it’s a little treacherous. Nageru was moving forward and was about to kick a rock out of way when a snake reared up from a coil. Korroc took a step up and brought a hammer down on it. Caleth and Thane were at the back and began moving forward, “What is going on?” Caleth called out. Nageru brought a fist down on the snake and it lashed out in retaliation, biting Nageru’s leg. Korroc brought his hammer down hard on the snake, hitting it hard enough to crush it. 
Moving on, they managed to clear a path for the injured and make it through the cave and to a narrower area on the other side. Moving forward, they come to an area that is split slightly with a cave off the passage. The cave was large, thirty feet high and more than 60 feet deep and inside was a stone building, a shrine. Looking at the building and the marking, a 10 foot tall hammer carved into the side of the building. “Torag” he said and started moving down to it. Caleth told the injured to wait just a moment while they checked to see if it was safe. As Korroc and Nageru moved to the left, they suddenly heard a buzzing noise. Looking up, they realized above the shrine was a giant fly. The fly saw them immediately and moved down toward Korroc, acting very unhappy that anyone would disturb it’s cave. The fly attacked and Korroc got his shield up just in time. The fly grabbed hold of the shield and seemed attached, so Korroc swung his hammer and got a glancing blow on it. A second fly crawled to the edge of the shrine and buzzed down to the wall above Nageru. The fly attacked Nageru, but he was able to dodge to the side just as tried to strike. Nageru brought fists down on the fly. Caleth moved forward, unslinging his glaive and thrusting forward, puncturing the fly. Thane moved up and hit a fly with a dagger. The first fly tried to attack Korroc again, but could not, the fly seemed to be trying to pull the shield away from Korroc and that caused Korroc’s attack to miss as he tried to squash it with his hammer. The fly on Nageru hit him hard, but he was able to retaliate and squash the fly with his fists. Caleth yelled out “Turn around and I’ll hit it.” Thane, not able to get to the remaining fly, moved to the other side of the building to look for any more threats. He immediately saw a writhing mass of maggots at the other end of the building. The fly started pulling hard on Korroc’s shield, but he managed to keep hold of it. He swung his hammer and hit it again, but it still kept hold of his shield. Korroc managed to turn toward the group and yelled “Get this thing off me.” Nageru moved up and brought fists down on it and the fly collapsed. Korroc immediately reached out and touched Nageru to heal him. Thane moved back to the group “Maggots” he pointed, over there. Caleth went to the corner and looked for himself. “Three of them” he confirmed. Thane moved back to the corner behind Caleth, but a maggot noticed them at that point and moved to attack Caleth. Caleth was able to react and stab it with his glaive before the maggot was able to reach him, but the maggot pushed forward and tried to bite Caleth, but missed. The third maggot suddenly disappeared into a hole in the ground. Another one started moving their way as well. Hearing the commotion, Korroc began moving that way. Caleth jabbed at the maggot and skewered it. Thane moved up to the one that had wiggled their way and tried to stab it but missed. Korroc moved up even with him and brought his hammer down for a glancing blow. The third maggot suddenly burst from the ground behind Caleth, but missed a bite. Nageru took advantage of that and brought his fists down on the maggot that just popped up, squashing the maggot. Caleth pushed the glaive between the two dwarves to stab the maggot from reach, but missed, so Thane stabbed it and it stopped moving. 
The group moved to the stone shrine door. Korroc moved to the door of his god’s shrine and tried the door, it was stuck fast. He put his back to it and pulled hard, slowing moving the door. Stepping in he looked around and found a pool of water, looking closely, he realized it was pristine. “Holy water,” he muttered and collected some of it. Anevia exploded on him, calling him worthless and saying that “It doesn’t surprise me that a man like  you would say something like that, always creeping around and hiding his face from people.” Thane put a hand her shoulder to calm her and Nageru looked at Horgus and said “You can go and go without us if you are in such a hurry, we don’t mind.”  Caleth moved forward with his hands raised, ready to try to calm things down. After eight hours, he was confident that the shrine was a safe place and he leaned against the bench and fell asleep. Everyone let Korroc sleep so that he could regain his spells and recover. When Korroc finally awoke, he turned and began his morning prayers and preparation. As he stood everyone in the area suddenly felt a wave of energy flow over them. Korroc smiled and nodded to himself, immediately realizing that Torag had blessed them all for the work done on the shrine.
