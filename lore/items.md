# Significant Items

Items of importance to the campaign — magical, plot-relevant, or symbolic. Update as new items appear.

## The Silver Scales of Terendelev

**Origin:** Cast off by the silver dragon Terendelev as her last act before death. She used her final magic to slow the party's fall into the rift; the scales scattered across the underdark cavern at the same time.

**Description:** Curved plates of metallic silver, each the size of a man's hand, some still tipped with dried blood. Warm to the touch. Carry a faint sense of Terendelev's last will: *get them out, get them out, get them out.*

**Properties:**
- Each surviving party member picked one up.
- Each scale carries **one charge of a unique ability,** specific to the bearer's nature and need.
- The scales seem to **regenerate their charge** (this has been functioning as such in play — confirm with GM).

**Assigned charges:**
- **Korroc's scale:** Feather Fall / Levitate. Used to descend gently in Chapter 1.
- **Caleth's scale:** Unspent. He is deliberately holding it for something specific. Terendelev's recognition of him suggests a unique purpose.
- **Thane's scale:** Levitation. Used to ascend the rat-hole in Chapter 3.
- **Nageru's scale:** Divine smite empowerment for one fight. Fists count as good-aligned and magical, overcoming demonic resistances. Used against the dretches in Chapter 3.

**Symbolic significance:** The scales are Terendelev's blessing on the party. While they carry them, she is *with* them. They are the campaign's first holy relic.

## Radiance — The Sword of Yaniel

**Origin:** The longsword carried by the paladin Yaniel during the Fourth Mendevian Crusade. She fell at the gates of Drezen carrying it. The sword was thought lost for half a century until a salvage team recovered it and placed it in the Kenabres museum.

**Description:** Razor-sharp longsword of superior craftsmanship. Blade pale and clean as if newly forged. Crossguard a sweep of silver-and-gold Iomedaen sunwork. Pommel a small carved sun. Carries a **strong magical glow** visible to anyone who can see magic.

**Properties:**
- Holy weapon, almost certainly **+1 or +2 holy** at minimum
- Likely has additional powers that activate as the bearer attunes (paladin levels, alignment, or specific moments)
- Recognized Thane's bloody hand with a faint *acknowledging* warmth when he touched it in Chapter 3 — suggesting the sword recognizes lawful good intent

**Recent history:** Stolen from the Kenabres museum approximately two months before the campaign began by Templars of the Ivory Labyrinth, who intended to **corrupt** it for their own purposes per the S. V. letter.

**Current custody:** **Caleth.** The blade **chose him in Chapter 6** — it woke in his hand at the Defender's Heart and reshaped into a **glaive**, and has not changed back for him since. (It takes a different form for whoever holds it: a longsword for Nageru, a warhammer for Korroc, a glaive for Caleth — each sized to the wielder's reach.) Korroc returned it to him with plain reasoning: the blade keeps finding your hand. Caleth has carried and fought with it through Chapters 6–7, its holy edge blazing white on a smite. **No longer with Thane.**

**Symbolic significance:** The first relic the party has rescued *from* the enemy. Yaniel's legacy now partly rests on whoever carries it. Returning Radiance to lawful use is a meaningful moral statement.

## The Adamantine Warhammer of the First Crusade (Korroc's)

**Origin:** A masterwork warhammer kept in the Gray Garrison's museum of First Crusade relics. The demons holding the garrison had used it to finish defiling the bodies of two slain Iomedaen knights.

**Description:** A beautifully balanced warhammer, the head a deep blue-black — **adamantine**, the unbreakable metal — and lighter than steel its size has any right to be. **Enchanted** (radiates magic; exact properties TBD).

**Recovered:** Chapter 7. Korroc named the metal at a touch, gave thanks to **both Torag and Iomedae**, cleaned the defilement from it, and took it up as his own. **Now his primary weapon.**

**Symbolic significance:** A First Crusade relic put back into a righteous hand — and for the dwarf who lifts it, the deep-blue sheen of adamantine carries an unspoken echo of Khar-Zadûn.

## The Iomedae Sunburst Shield (Korroc's, borrowed)

**Origin:** From the sealed temple armory of Iomedae inside the Gray Garrison.

**Description:** An old, well-made shield bearing Iomedae's sunburst, worked by a craftsman's hand. **Radiates magic.**

**Recovered:** Chapter 7. Korroc — a paladin of **Torag** — took it up **borrowed and with respect**, carrying Iomedae's sign through the garrison *"until I can ask the gods' leave to put her sunburst on alongside Torag's hammer and anvil."*

**Symbolic significance:** A paladin of one god honoring another's; a small ecumenical grace amid the desecration.

## The Othirubo Sword (once Irabeth's)

**Origin:** A fine longsword found in a weapon case in the Gray Garrison alchemist's workshop, the Othirubo letter laid across it.

**Description:** A well-kept longsword, neglected under a film of dust. Per the letter, it **once belonged to Irabeth**, who sold it to Kandro Nyserian years ago; it was bound as a gift for **Lord Staunton's brother**.

**Recovered:** Chapter 7. **Caleth** took the case into his keeping. *Irabeth may want to know it has surfaced.*

**Symbolic significance:** Ties the party's ally Irabeth to the enemy's correspondence — a small mystery (why did a paladin sell this blade?) and a deliberate cruelty (Nyserian was skinned for being slow to deliver it). See **The Othirubo Letter**.

## The Othirubo Letter

**Origin:** Found with the sword above in the Gray Garrison, Chapter 7.

**Full text:**

> *Lord Staunton,*
>
> *I believe you will find the enclosed weapon to be a singular and familiar delight, for it once belonged to your old "friend" Irabeth. She sold it to Kandro Nyserian, of all people—I've no idea why. Kandro's been sitting on it for years—still would be, except his home got smashed by an ulkreth. Shame. He managed to save the sword and came here, begging for an escort north to Drezen, doubtless so he could give you the sword and weasel some cash to help him set up a new home. I hope you don't begrudge a creativity I took—our friend Nyserian serves now as a warm suit for a vermlek as punishment for only revealing the sword now instead of when he first acquired it. I suspect the blade will take to your brother's touch nicely!*
>
> *your loyal servant,*
> *Othirubo*

**Key intelligence:**
- A **Lord Staunton** holds authority here and is bound for **Drezen** — strongly echoing **S. V.**, who the earlier letter said *"will assume command of Drezen."* (The party has filed the Staunton / S. V. resonance; it is **NOT** confirmed in-world.)
- Staunton has a **brother**, for whom the sword was intended.
- **Othirubo** is a cruel servant of Staunton's, comfortable arranging demonic "creativity."
- **Confirms Kandro Nyserian's fate:** skinned and worn by a **vermlek** demon as punishment — the very skinsuit-demon the party killed at the Gray Garrison doors in Chapter 6.

**Current custody:** Thane holds the note; Caleth holds the sword.

## The S. V. Letter

**Origin:** A folded parchment letter found in Hosilla's belt-pouch after her death in Chapter 3.

**Full text:**

> *Hosilla,*
>
> *You will remain, for the time being, in Kenabres, but know this: the city's days are numbered. Seek a place of safety—the underground den of your mongrel lackeys should suffice to keep you safe from the devastation to come. I shall assume command of Drezen shortly, and once Vorlesh has finished with the wardstone and Kenabres is no longer of interest to us, you are to return to my side. Excellent news regarding the salvage of Yaniel's sword from the museum as well—bring it with you, for I believe this weapon could be quite useful once we corrupt it. Before you leave for Drezen, stop by the three safe houses (Nyserian Manor, Topaz Solutions, and the Tower of Estrod—the passphrase remains "I've new material for the archives," for now) to ensure no evidence remains behind. May Lord Deskari and Lord Baphomet watch over you!*
>
> *S. V.*

**Key intelligence:**
- The wardstone fall was **deliberate** and planned in advance
- **S. V.** is taking command of **Drezen**
- **Vorlesh** is the operative who destroyed the wardstone
- Three safe houses in Kenabres: **Nyserian Manor, Topaz Solutions, Tower of Estrod**
- Passphrase: **"I've new material for the archives"**
- Templars serve both **Lord Deskari** and **Lord Baphomet**
- Hosilla was instructed to **destroy evidence** at the safe houses before traveling to Drezen — meaning the party has a window before the safe houses are sterilized

**Current custody:** **Thane**, carried against his skin inside his chain shirt.

**Plot importance:** **The single most important piece of evidence the party has acquired.** This letter, delivered to Eagle Watch leadership, will rewrite the surface crusade's understanding of the Templar threat.

## The Estrod Records & the Minagho Letter

**Origin:** Recovered from the chest in the Baphomet chapel beneath the Tower of Estrod (Chapter 5).

**The records:** A bundle of years of Templar operations — lists of agents in Kenabres and other cities, supply caches, a diagram of the (former) wardstone network with crabbed annotations, plans, routes, schedules. A major intelligence haul, second only to the S. V. letter.

**The Minagho letter (full text):**

> *Faxon,*
>
> *You did well to report your superior's mishandling of funds, and I trust that you will continue to serve as loyally in the weeks to come. You need not command the Kenabres Templars for long, for the city is about to die — I only wish I could be there to take part in its murder, for I have fond memories of my Red Morning Massacre. No matter, I suppose, for your mortal kin will all fall soon enough — as I said, Kenabres's days are short.*
>
> *Vorlesh has already left to meet with our Lord's daughter in the Abyss to secure a Nahyndrian crystal of the proper purity, and once she has what she needs, she'll arrive in Kenabres to finish the job and turn the wardstones fully to our use. You will know when she succeeds, I suspect!*
>
> *Praise Lord Baphomet!*
>
> *Minagho*

**Key intelligence:**
- **Vorlesh** is in the Abyss securing a **Nahyndrian crystal** ("of the proper purity") to "turn the wardstones fully to our use" — confirms and predates Irabeth's Ch 6 briefing.
- **Minagho** (demon of Raliscradin) is coordinating; **Faxon** is the new Kenabres Templar commander, elevated for informing on his superior.
- Names a "**Red Morning Massacre**" in Minagho's past.

**Current custody:** Carried by the party (Caleth/Thane), to be delivered to Irabeth with the rest of the intelligence.

## Unholy Symbols of Baphomet

**Origin:** Recovered from dead Templar operatives in the underdark.

**Description:** Black iron bull-headed pendants with long curving horns. The standard symbol of the Demon Prince Baphomet. Some are simple worn pendants; others are more elaborate ritual pieces.

**Current count:**
- Two recovered (one from the lone wizard at the campfire in Session 2, one from the dead Templar at the spore cougher in Session 2)
- Both wrapped in cloth and carried separately. They have a way of *listening,* and the party does not want them open in their packs.

**Current custody:** Caleth has one; Thane has one. Both wrapped.

**Plot use:** Evidence to deliver to the surface; also a curse-prevention measure to keep them from being recovered by demonic agents.

**Current location:** **Thane's home**, presumably in Kenabres. He has not carried it into the underdark.

## The Three Safe Houses (All Cleared)

**Locations in Kenabres:**

1. **Nyserian Manor** — ~~Property of an unknown noble family. Possibly Hosilla's own residence or her cover identity.~~ **DESTROYED (Chapter 4).** A demon's path ran straight through the property. The manor is unsalvageable. Whatever it held was lost in the collapse. **Off the list.** (Its owner, **Kandro Nyserian**, later turned up skinned and worn by a vermlek at the Gray Garrison — see npcs.md.)
2. **Topaz Solutions** — A merchant front (flowers-and-beaker sign). **CLEARED (Chapter 5).** The Templars laid an ambush; below the shop, a poisoner's still-room with a Baphomet shrine, a clockwork sword-trap (Thane snatched the vapor bottle from it), and a root-draining bark-child creature. A chest of five **poisoned** Iomedae holy symbols and a taunting note pointed the party onward to the Tower of Estrod. **Off the list.**
3. **The Tower of Estrod** — A sheared-off stone tower-stump. **CLEARED (Chapter 5).** Poisoned (via the vapor bottle saved from Topaz) and stormed; reinforcements and a fire-wielding tiefling leader fought; a Baphomet chapel below held the chest with years of Templar records and the **Minagho letter** (see *The Estrod Records & the Minagho Letter* above). **Off the list.**

**Passphrase:** "I've new material for the archives." (Used by the party to bluff entry at Estrod in Ch 5.)

**Status:** **All three safe houses are now addressed** — Nyserian Manor destroyed (Ch 4); Topaz Solutions and Tower of Estrod cleared (Ch 5). The remaining value is the *intelligence* hauled out of them (agent lists, the wardstone-network diagram, the Minagho letter), the party's to deliver to Irabeth.

**Plot use:** The location arc is spent — but the recovered records open new threads: named agents, **Minagho**, **Faxon**, and Vorlesh's Abyss errand for the Nahyndrian crystal.
