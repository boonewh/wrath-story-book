# Factions

The major organizations, tribes, and powers relevant to the campaign so far.

---

## The Mendevian Crusade

**Type:** The standing military and religious force defending the surface world from the Worldwound.

**Status:** Ongoing for nearly a century. The current war is the **Fifth Crusade** — though it has stretched on so long that crusaders no longer count campaigns the way they once did.

**Faith:** Primarily devoted to Iomedae (the inheritor of Aroden's mantle), though it includes paladins, clerics, and warriors of many lawful good faiths. Torag, Shelyn, and other allied gods are represented.

**Headquarters:** Various command-houses across Mendev, with major presence at Kenabres, Nerosyan, and several frontier fortresses.

**Key elements:**
- The **Wardstones** — magical stones at fortifications that prevent demons from crossing the Worldwound border. Kenabres's wardstone was at **the Kite**, the fortress destroyed at the start of the campaign. Its destruction is the campaign's inciting catastrophe.
- The crusade has been bleeding for years. Morale is low. Recruitment has stretched into questionable territory. Armasse is the holiday that keeps the public spirit alive.

**Plot relevance:** The party will likely become important figures in the surface crusade as the campaign progresses.

---

## The Eagle Watch

**Type:** Specialized military intelligence and counter-infiltration order operating within the Mendevian Crusade.

**Mission:** Internal security. Identifying and rooting out demon-cult infiltration of crusader ranks and Kenabrian institutions.

**Method:** Combination of internal investigation, contracted agents, and elite combat operatives. Maintains a low public profile.

**Key facts:**
- **Thane and Anevia are contracted agents** of the Eagle Watch — NOT full members. They were hired three months before the campaign opened.
- **Irabeth** is named by Anevia as the surface contact they must reach with their findings. She is likely Eagle Watch leadership or closely allied.
- The Eagle Watch suspected Templar infiltration for at least six months before the campaign started. They did not have proof. The party now has proof.

**Plot relevance:** Likely a major early-campaign ally and political anchor. May become a recurring institutional presence as the campaign develops.

## The Templars of the Ivory Labyrinth

**Type:** Demon-sworn cult. Servants of **Baphomet,** the Lord of Beasts and Demon Prince of Minotaurs, master of the Ivory Labyrinth.

**Mission:** Infiltrate, corrupt, and destroy from within. They pose as crusaders, priests, scholars, and merchants while serving Baphomet and (in the larger plot) **Deskari**, the Lord of the Locust Host.

**Methods:**
- Wear crusader colors openly while serving the demonic lords
- Infiltrate orders, brand the bull's-head of Baphomet on their bare chests beneath their tabards
- Maintain safe houses across cities
- Coordinate via written letters and verbal passphrases
- Summon and bind lesser demons for use in their plots
- Steal and corrupt holy relics

**Known members (from the campaign so far):**
- **Hosilla** (dead, Chapter 3) — Templar contact in Kenabres, killed by Korroc's hammer
- **S. V.** (unknown identity) — Hosilla's superior; will assume command of Drezen
- **Vorlesh** — The operative who "finished with the wardstone"; agent of the wardstone's destruction
- Several unnamed Templars killed in the underdark — the wizard at the campfire, the two at the spore cougher, the two in the ritual chamber

**Known safe houses in Kenabres:**
- **Nyserian Manor** — *DESTROYED (Chapter 4).* A demon's path ran straight through the property; the manor is unsalvageable. One safe house off the list without the party having to fight for it.
- **Topaz Solutions**
- **The Tower of Estrod**

**Passphrase (per S. V. letter):** *"I've new material for the archives."*

**Material in their possession (recovered):**
- The S. V. letter (now in Thane's chain shirt)
- Yaniel's sword Radiance (now in Thane's hands — the Templars had intended to corrupt it)
- Multiple unholy symbols of Baphomet (party has two wrapped in cloth)

**Plot relevance:** The campaign's primary early-arc antagonists. The party is now in possession of evidence that should, when delivered to the surface, allow the surface crusade to roll up the entire Kenabres operation. Expect Drezen to become a major mid-campaign target.

## Servants of Baphomet (the Larger Cult)

**Demon Lord:** Baphomet, Prince of Minotaurs, Lord of the Ivory Labyrinth.

**Cult activities:**
- Ritual murder at killing-tables (as found in Chapter 3)
- Summoning of lesser demons (dretches found in the underdark)
- Corruption of holy relics
- Long-term infiltration plots

**Allied demonic forces:** Deskari (Lord of the Locust Host) — the senior demonic power behind the wardstone plot, per the S. V. letter ("May Lord Deskari and Lord Baphomet watch over you").

**Plot relevance:** Baphomet is one of the campaign's major late-game antagonists; the party may face him directly at full mythic power.

## Vescavor Tribe

**Type:** Hostile mongrelfolk tribe in the Kenabres underdark, allied with the Templars.

**Description:** Like Nathholm, the Vescavor are descendants of the touched First Crusade survivors who fled below. Unlike Nathholm, they have **embraced** the demonic taint in their blood and made common cause with the surface cultists who can supply them.

**Features:**
- Built barricades and defensive positions in their tunnels
- Maintain dens with sleeping arrangements, pantries, and crude infrastructure
- Have a **maze** built in the old Kenabres cisterns where they keep summoned demons
- A **killing-table chamber** for ritual murder

**Status after Session 3:** Most or all of the Vescavor in the immediate dens are dead. The chamber the party cleared was likely the operational center of the Kenabres-area Vescavor.

**Plot relevance:** Smaller-scale antagonists. Likely cleared as the party returns to the surface. Could potentially have splinter cells in deeper caverns.

## Nathholm (Mongrelfolk)

**Type:** Allied mongrelfolk village in the underdark.

**Leadership:** **Chief Sull** (see npcs.md).

**Population:** Some hundreds, dwelling on a rocky island in the center of a subterranean lake. Stone houses, lanterns, mismatched timber rafts.

**Origin:** Descendants of the touched First Crusade dead who came below the surface rather than face it. They have raised generations in the dark and built a culture of their own. They keep watch on the surface but do not interfere with it.

**Status:** **Allied with the party** after Session 2. Pledged to harry the Vescavor and stand against the Templar infiltration. Asked for mutual recognition from the surface.

**Plot relevance:** A potentially significant unconventional ally for the surface crusade. Their willingness to fight could open up entire fronts the crusade has not previously considered. Their existence as a sympathetic people is also a moral inflection point for any character (Horgus, for example) who has dismissed them as monsters.

## The Mordant Spire (Half-elven)

**Type:** The legendary half-elven stronghold tied to the elder elven mysteries.

**Description:** A community of half-elves who maintain physical and academic training of extraordinary depth. Spireborn agents are sometimes sent into the world on long missions whose purposes are rarely shared with those agents. The Spire keeps its own counsel and shares little with the wider world.

**Plot relevance for the campaign:**
- **Caleth is Spireborn by blood** — both his biological parents were Spireborn — **but he was not raised at the Spire.** His parents fled the Spire when he was an infant to join the Riftwardens (see entry below).
- Caleth visited the Spire briefly later in life (a "season," as he sometimes calls it) and there absorbed some martial techniques, a calligraphic hand, and certain formal mannerisms (the bow-for-elder-peers; the long-level look the Spire used on a student speaking out of turn). He has a sister-instructor he has not heard from in seven years.
- **The Spire is not directing his presence in Kenabres.** Whether the Spire's masters know what became of his Riftwarden parents — and whether they would want him back if they did — is unknown and likely unresolved until later in the campaign.

**Status:** Mysterious and largely uninvolved with the active campaign. Likely to become more relevant as Caleth's Riftwarden-orphan arc develops and the question of his parents' fate sharpens.

> **NOTE (file pass, May 2026):** Earlier versions of this entry stated that Caleth was *sent to Kenabres on a Spire mission* and that *the Spire's masters may have known about the wardstone plot.* **Both were fabrications by a previous Claude.** Corrected: Caleth's biological parents are Riftwardens (vanished into the Worldwound when he was less than a month old); Caleth was raised in Kenabres by human foster parents and mentored by the Lantern Seer (see characters/npcs.md). The Spire is a lineage source and a brief later-life teacher, not his upbringing or his current command structure.

## The Riftwardens

**Type:** Secretive arcane order. Guardians of places where reality thins — portals, rifts, planar weaknesses, and the Worldwound itself.

**Mission:** Watch the wound. Close what can be closed. Identify and counter cult activity around portal-magic. They are *not* a public organization; most crusaders have never heard of them.

**Mark:** The **Seeker's Spiral** — a spiraling sigil carried by members and (sometimes) by their bloodline. Not a tattoo. Not a scar. Something *deeper.*

**Plot relevance for the campaign:**
- **Caleth's biological parents were Riftwardens** who vanished into the Worldwound on an unexplained mission less than a month after Caleth's birth.
- **Caleth carries the Seeker's Spiral on his shoulder blade** — a birthmark since the day he was born, marking him as a Riftwarden's child. His campaign trait is **Riftwarden Orphan.**
- **Aravashniel is a Riftwarden elder.** He recognized Caleth's lineage from the day they met in the underdark. The whispered conversation at Gwerm Manor in Chapter 4 was where Aravashniel revealed his identity to Caleth. *(See characters/npcs.md.)*
- **Blackwing Library was a Riftwarden stronghold** — the order's working library, holding the most extensive writings on demons in Golarion. **Destroyed before the party reached it in Chapter 4.** Aravashniel's grief at finding it in ruins is for the order's loss, not only the books.
- **Most of the party still does not know about the Riftwardens.** As of Chapter 4, Caleth has learned that Aravashniel is one — that secret is now shared between them, kept from Korroc, Thane, Nageru, Anevia, Horgus, and Klareth.
- Somewhere within the Worldwound lies the truth of Caleth's parents' fate. Possibly alive. Possibly ended by something or someone who still walks. This is one of Caleth's primary drivers, beneath the Shelynian surface.

**Status:** Active but invisible. The party may encounter Riftwardens (or those who recognize the Spiral) once they reach the surface, or in the deeper Worldwound campaigns.

> **NOTE (file pass, May 2026):** Riftwardens were not previously documented in this file. They are core to Caleth's actual character canon (per his sheet) and should now be treated as a real, active faction whose interests intersect with the party's, especially as the Worldwound arc deepens.

## Khar-Zadûn (Skyguard of)

**Type:** The legendary **Dwarven Sky City** — torn from the heavens during the first demonic invasions of antiquity.

**History:** Once a cloud-piercing dwarven citadel with star-forges and runic wind-channels. Now lost — either fallen, sealed, or unreachable. The Skyguard were its elite defenders.

**Plot relevance:**
- **Thane's father Thorek Stonevein was a Skyguard.** Thorek dreamed of reclaiming Khar-Zadûn before he died in the Fourth Crusade.
- The sky-steel insignia of the Skyguard is the family relic in Thane's home.
- **Khar-Zadûn itself is a possible major late-campaign location.** If Thane ever stands in his father's city, it should be a defining moment of the entire campaign.

**Status:** Lost. Not yet relevant in active play, but seeded.

## The Worldwound

**Type:** The great Abyssal rift in the world. A bleeding wound in reality from which demons pour into Golarion.

**Geography:** Vast region to the north and west of Mendev, formerly the kingdom of Sarkoris. Now demon-haunted ruin.

**History:** Opened nearly a century ago when Aroden's death weakened the planar barriers. The Mendevian Crusades have held the demons back from the rest of Golarion ever since — barely.

**Status:** With Kenabres's wardstone destroyed, the Worldwound's pressure on the eastern front has dramatically increased. The Storm King's attack may be the opening of a major demonic offensive.

**Plot relevance:** The campaign's primary setting and primary antagonist as a place. The party's ultimate goal is, eventually, the closing of the Worldwound itself.

---

## The Sunken Fist (Monastery-Fortress of Irori)

**Type:** Secluded monastery-fortress of Irori-worshippers, nestled in the sparsely populated foothills bordering the Worldwound.

**Description:** A bastion of the Master of Masters at the edge of demon-haunted territory. The order practices a unique martial discipline in which *each strike and block is considered a prayer in motion.* They train monks (often Scaled Fist) and paladins (notably Lawbringer-Aasimar paladins), and host a small population of lay-members serving the monastery.

**Plot relevance for the campaign:**
- **Nageru was born here.** His parents — Elara (scribe) and Kaelen (groundskeeper-artisan) — are still serving the order in late years.
- **Master Borin** (human Monk) and **Sister Lyra** (Aasimar Paladin) trained him. Both still living, presumed still at the monastery.
- **Nageru was recognized as a Lawbringer** here. He left at age 20, "called toward the storm" of the Worldwound. His Armasse-day arrival in Kenabres was not coincidence.
- **A late-campaign possibility:** if the Sunken Fist comes under demonic siege as the Worldwound spreads, that is a wound for Nageru no celestial smite can close. A potential major emotional beat / location.

**Status:** Active but distant from current events. The order has not contacted Nageru since he left; he has not contacted them.

> **NOTE (file pass, May 2026):** The Sunken Fist was not previously documented. It is the canonical origin of Nageru per the player's character sheet and his approved background. Treat as real and reachable.
