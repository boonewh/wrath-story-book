# Campaign Timeline — Wrath of the Righteous

Running summary of what each session/chapter established, in order. **Update cadence:** append 2–4 sentences per new chapter during the canon pass (the README's "suggest sentences for timeline.md" step lands here). This is an internal reference file — it records some party-private threads; anything marked *(secret)* must never surface in the wrong POV's prose. When this file conflicts with a chapter, **the chapter wins** (canon hierarchy).

## Where We Are Now (end of Session 13)

The Fifth Crusade holds the southeast corner of Drezen's ruins and the Ahari Bridge. Paradise Hill is sealed, the cemetery is cleansed, and the army — ~200 soldiers plus ~200 freed and armed prisoners under Irabeth's command — is camped at the Ahari's south edge while engineers shore the span. The citadel stands in open sight, holding the Sword of Valor and Staunton Vhane. The party is **Level 8, Mythic Tier 2**. A saboteur (prime suspect: Nurah, unproven) is quiet but loose in the company. The enemy has been confirmed to command mythic power of its own.

## Book 1 — The Worldwound Incursion (Kenabres)

**Session 1 / Chapter 1 — The Hammer in the Deep (Korroc POV).** At the Armasse festival, Kenabres's wardstone falls and the Storm King Khorramzadeh attacks; the silver dragon **Terendelev** reveals herself, fights, and dies buying the city time. Her last magic drops the four (and Anevia, Aravashnial, Horgus) into the underdark rifts instead of to their deaths, and each of the four carries away **a silver scale holding one charge of her dying gift**. Korroc finds and consecrates a lost shrine of Torag; Torag answers. The long climb out begins.

**Session 2 / Chapter 2 — The Glaive in the Gentle Hand (Caleth POV).** The survivors cross the underdark: Nageru saves Horgus one-handed at the chasm; the party digs Crel out of a collapsed tower; the mongrelfolk village of **Nathholm** (Chief Sull, Lann, Dyra) becomes an ally — the party promises to harry the Vescavor and carry word to the surface. A dead Templar's unholy symbol is the first physical proof of the infiltration. *(Secret: Caleth dreams of Terendelev on a cliff — "Be the kind one, Spireborn." He tells no one.)*

**Session 3 / Chapter 3 — The Knife in the Dark (Thane POV).** Vescavor barricade fights; Thane nearly dies twice (cave-lizard, and **Hosilla's** spinning glaive — she is killed by Korroc's hammer). From Hosilla's belt-pouch: the **S. V. letter** — three Templar safe houses in Kenabres (Nyserian Manor, Topaz Solutions, Tower of Estrod), a passphrase, Drezen named as the Templar destination, and **Vorlesh** credited with "finishing" the wardstone. **Radiance, Yaniel's stolen sword, is recovered.** Thane quietly reveals his Eagle Watch contract (and Anevia as partner-not-lover) to Caleth.

**Session 4 / Chapter 4 — The Smith in the Ruin (Korroc POV).** The party surfaces into a burning, demon-ridden Kenabres. Corrupted Sarenrae knights are stopped mid-sacrifice and the orphaned child **Klarah** joins the company; her parents are found dead. At Irabeth's house a half-orc Templar agent is killed and **Anevia's marriage to Irabeth** is revealed. At the ruined Blackwing Library, Caleth is struck dead and **Korroc's first Life Oracle fire-elemental transformation** brings him back — the first hint of what is coming. *(Secret — Caleth only: Aravashnial reveals himself as a Riftwarden ELDER; the Blackwing was a Riftwarden stronghold; he recognized Caleth's lineage from the start.)*

**Session 5 / Chapter 5 — The Patient Thunder / The Open Fist (Nageru POV).** Nageru leads the manor group through a fleeing demon-swarm to the reunion at the Blackwing, then the **Defender's Heart**. The S. V. letter goes to Irabeth, who names the traitor she once caught and lost: **Staunton Vhane** (whether he is "S. V." stays unconfirmed for now). Two safe houses fall: **Topaz Solutions** and the **Tower of Estrod**, yielding years of Templar records and the **Minagho letter** — Vorlesh has gone to the Abyss for a Nahyndrian crystal to corrupt the wardstones.

**Session 6 / Chapter 6 — The Blade That Chooses (Caleth POV).** **Radiance chooses Caleth** — waking as a glaive in his hands after showing Korroc a hammer (Korroc gives it up for the healer's place) and being declined by Nageru. Quednys supplies the **rod of cancellation**. Vermlek demons wearing the skins of Kandro Nyserian and **Lord Hulrun** are destroyed at the bookshop and the Gray Garrison doors — "Demons. Literally wearing their skins."

**Session 7 / Chapter 7 — The Threshold and the Bar / No Honest Tenants (Thane POV).** The **Gray Garrison** is breached and cleared floor by floor. Korroc cleans a fouled shrine of Iomedae and claims the **adamantine First Crusade warhammer**; a Celestial passphrase opens the temple armory. The **Othirubo letter** names "Lord Staunton." Nageru is run through and brought back. The wardstone fragment is close.

**Session 8 / Chapter 8 — The Stone Remembers (Korroc POV).** Caleth drives the rod of cancellation into the caged **wardstone fragment**: the golden explosion makes the four **MYTHIC** — Vorlesh's plan to transform the crusade into half-fiends is foiled at the same stroke. The wardstone visions confirm **S. V. = Staunton Vhane**; Korroc and Thane privately see Staunton at their fathers' table *(the personal layer stays theirs)*. Iomedae remakes Korroc's borrowed shield with **Torag's hammer-and-anvil**. Thane: "When we find him, he is a dead man."

**Session 8.5 / Chapter 8.5 — Interlude: The Breathing Space (ensemble).** Downtime weeks. Korroc rebuilds a forge and restores the two broken helms; Horgus's hidden cellar feeds the city and his prayer-beads go to Nageru; Thane secretly scouts south and returns half-dead with the news: **the Mendevian host is coming, led by Queen Galfrey.**

## Book 2 — Sword of Valor (the road to Drezen)

**Session 9 / Chapter 9 — The Voice That Answers (Nageru POV).** **Queen Galfrey** arrives; the wardstones are gone everywhere, and "Iomedae has seen fit that you will be their replacement." The four are knighted **Knights of the Fifth Crusade** (with medals; Irabeth knighted too) and given the Drezen mission: reclaim the citadel and the **Sword of Valor** while Aponavicius's army is away. The company forms — Irabeth commanding, Anevia, Horgus (logistics), Aravashnial (eye regenerated at Nageru's request), plus the Queen's three: **Aron Kir, Sosiel Vaenic, Nurah Dendiwhar**. March north: Valas's Gift stands empty; the tiefling camp at **Vilareth Ford** is destroyed and its prisoners freed; a survivor warns of a bigger army at **Keeper's Canyon** — which falls in turn (~nine crusaders lost).

**Session 10 / Chapter 10 — The House That Beauty Built (Caleth POV).** On the march: a **sawn wagon axle** — the first sabotage. A cliff-top **Chapel of Shelyn**, desecrated by Kabriri's ghoul-cult, is cleared (gargoyles, ghouls, a teleporting demon frozen by Caleth's inverted rays) and **re-consecrated — Radiance gains power**. That night, **shadowblood is found planted in Thane's pack** (second sabotage); the camp-wide evil-sweep finds nothing.

**Session 11 / Chapter 11 — The Latch That Held / What the Stone Remembers (Thane POV).** The **Vescavor queen** beneath the Ahari Gorge is destroyed; at the Abyssal rift behind her nest, three vrocks come through and **Aravashnial closes the rift — publicly revealing himself a Riftwarden** *(his elder rank, the Caleth connection, and that Caleth knew since Ch 4 stay secret)*. Topside, **Aron Kir is knifed by the unseen saboteur** — gnome-sized footprints; **Nurah is confronted and nothing is proven**. Sosiel delivers the Queen's sealed satchel: the **reconstructed Drezen blueprints**, signed seventy years ago by twelve dwarves — the first four names the Stonevein parents: Thorek, Borin, Dagna, Helja. The map goes into Caleth's spellbook; the traitor must never learn it exists.

**Session 12 / Chapter 12 — The Strain the Smith Takes (Korroc POV).** The outer city's southeast corner falls before dawn: the watchtower (schir demons and a vrock that saw through invisibility), **~200 prisoners freed at Paradise Hill**, and the **unnamed bridge south of the hill** taken — the enemy camps on its south side routed, Paradise Hill sealed (nothing reaches it without wings or magic). Irabeth's army camps on the captured ground: **a foothold on Drezen's edge — NOT the siege**. Ahari Bridge stays demon-held; the freed men arm themselves from a recovered stash of crusader gear and their sworn leader is raised over them; the cemetery is named the next target. *(This session's geography was corrected post-publication — see memory: drezen-geography-session12.)*

**Session 13 / Chapter 13 — The Sound the Thunder Makes (Nageru POV).** Korroc breaks the war-council tie against his own argument ("Cemetery. I won't lock us up"); Thane's walkout scout discovers **four demon-fused aurochs chained to Ahari Bridge's piers as living demolition**. The **cemetery's curse is broken** — ghouls, a berbalang, and a desecrated Iomedaean mausoleum re-hallowed by Caleth's dispel — and the **Ahari is cleared**: handlers dropped, an enemy fireball returned as ice, every chain shattered. A **mythic-enhanced three-headed chimera** (ice breath; Korroc's curse bites deep — and cold wards prove useless on him; he severs his life-link to stay standing; Caleth is bitten unconscious) is killed by Thane's daggers. The army moves to the Ahari's south edge; engineers shore the span. Aravashnial's verdict over the carcass: enhanced **"in the same way the stone affected you"** — the enemy has mythic power. **Party reaches Level 8, Mythic Tier 2.**
