# The Worldwound

The bleeding rift in the world. The campaign's primary antagonist as a place. The wound the Mendevian Crusade has been bleeding to staunch for nearly a century.

---

## Origin

When **Aroden died** roughly a century ago, the planar barriers of the world weakened. In the northwestern region of what was once the kingdom of **Sarkoris**, the Abyss itself tore through reality, creating a vast gateway through which demons could pour into the mortal world.

This is the Worldwound.

It has not closed. It cannot be closed by ordinary means. It has bled demons into Golarion ever since.

---

## Geography

**Location:** Northwestern Mendev / Eastern former Sarkoris. A vast region of demon-haunted ruin, several hundred miles across in either direction.

**Terrain:** What was once verdant kingdom is now twisted by Abyssal influence — forests of bleeding trees, rivers running with foul liquids, sky perpetually overcast with churning storms, ruined Sarkorian settlements occupied by demonic warlords.

**Borders:** Held — barely — by the **wardstone network** along the Mendevian frontier. Kenabres was one of the major eastern anchor cities.

## The Demonic Forces

### Major Demon Lords with Campaign Relevance

**Deskari — Lord of the Locust Host**
- Senior demonic power behind the wardstone destruction (per the S. V. letter)
- The Worldwound's most active demon prince
- Long-game architect of the campaign's central conspiracy
- Likely a late-campaign antagonist at full mythic scale

**Baphomet — Prince of Minotaurs, Lord of the Ivory Labyrinth**
- The Templars of the Ivory Labyrinth serve him directly
- Cultists wear his bull-headed brand on their bare chests beneath their tabards
- Master of mazes, manipulation, and corruption
- Mid-to-late campaign antagonist
- Holds the Ivory Labyrinth — a vast planar maze in the Abyss
- The party may eventually have to enter the Labyrinth itself

**Khorramzadeh — The Storm King**
- A Balor Lord — among the most powerful demons short of demon princes
- Personally led the attack on Kenabres at Armasse
- Killed Terendelev with a single sword-stroke
- Wields a flaming sword and a flaming whip
- Three times the height of a man, skin of fire and lightning
- Late-campaign personal antagonist; the party will likely face him at mythic level

### Lesser Demons Encountered So Far

**Dretches** (encountered in the maze, Session 3)
- The lowest-rank demons in the Abyssal hierarchy
- Quivering pillar-bodies of curdled flesh on knotted muscle
- Claws, bite, and a fear-howl aura
- Heal rapidly from normal weapon damage; require holy or magical attacks
- Used as expendable summons by Templar cultists

### Demons Likely to Be Encountered Later
- Glabrezu, vrocks, hezrous, succubi, marilith, balors (lesser than Khorramzadeh)
- Various cultist-summoned demons
- Eventually: demon lords directly

## The Mendevian Crusades

### The First Crusade
- Launched within years of the Worldwound's opening
- Pushed the demons back from spreading further east
- Established the original wardstone network
- Many First Crusade dead became the touched — those who took Abyssal taint and fled below the surface, becoming the ancestors of the mongrelfolk (see kenabres.md and factions.md)

### The Second Crusade
- Stabilized the borders
- Established the major crusader cities
- Founded military orders

### The Third Crusade
- A push to retake **Drezen** — a major fortress city deep in the Worldwound territory
- Initially successful, then catastrophically defeated
- Drezen has been in demonic hands ever since

### The Fourth Crusade
- A holding action that became a disaster
- **Thorek Stonevein died here**, as did the paladin **Yaniel** at Drezen's gates
- Severe casualties throughout the Mendevian forces
- Morale never fully recovered

### The Fifth Crusade
- Ongoing at campaign start
- Has been ongoing so long that crusaders no longer count campaigns the way they once did
- More a permanent military state than a discrete operation
- The campaign opens with this crusade in deep trouble — and the wardstone destruction makes everything worse

## Khar-Zadûn — The Lost Sky City

**Critical for Thane's arc.**

**What it was:**
- The legendary Dwarven Sky City
- Torn from the heavens during the first demonic invasions (predating even Aroden's death — older than the Worldwound itself, in some lore)
- Cloud-piercing towers
- Star-forges
- Halls with wind-channels that sang runic music
- Defended by the elite Skyguard (Thorek's order)

**What it is now:**
- Fallen, sealed, or unreachable
- Possibly in the Worldwound itself
- Possibly in a planar pocket
- Possibly under the Worldwound
- *Status: GM to determine*

**Plot relevance:**
- Thane's father dreamed of reclaiming it
- The Skyguard insignia is the family relic
- The line in Thane's song — *"I'll lay your sky-steel insignia / on the chain when the chain is free"* — points to a climactic moment involving Khar-Zadûn
- If Thane stands in his father's lost city, that should be one of the defining moments of the campaign

## The Wardstones

**Function:** Magical anchors at fortified locations along the Mendevian border. Each suppresses the local demonic incursion within its region.

**Status of the Network:**
- **Kenabres wardstone: DESTROYED** (Armasse, Session 1)
- Other wardstones: presumably still functional, but **weakened** by the network damage
- The Templars' coordination implies **other wardstones may be targeted soon**

**Strategic Importance:**
With Kenabres's wardstone gone, the eastern frontier is wide open. The party's mission to deliver intelligence and rally a defense is directly tied to whether Mendev can hold the line at all.

## What's Going to Happen Next

(GM/Author intent notes — for use by Claude when writing future chapters.)

The campaign will likely follow the broad arc of the published *Wrath of the Righteous* adventure path, with adaptations to honor the party's specific story:

1. **Session 4-10:** The party reaches the surface. Delivers the letter. Confronts the Templar safe houses. Defends Kenabres from continued demonic incursion. Likely meets Irabeth and key surface allies. Around session 10, the party becomes **Mythic** — gaining legendary powers and shifting the campaign's scope.

2. **Sessions 11-25:** The campaign to retake **Drezen** — the long march, the assault, the political consolidation of a crusader presence in former demon territory. S. V.'s identity is likely revealed in this arc.

3. **Sessions 26-40:** Mid-campaign — the war against the wider Templar conspiracy. Possible major confrontation with **Khorramzadeh** in this arc. Cultural and political work alongside military.

4. **Sessions 41-55:** The deeper Worldwound campaigns. The Ivory Labyrinth itself may be entered. **Baphomet** confronted. Khar-Zadûn may be reached. The party's mythic power approaches its peak.

5. **Sessions 56-60:** The final confrontation. **Deskari** and the closing of the Worldwound. The campaign's endgame.

Adapt freely as the players' choices and the GM's adaptations shape the actual story.
