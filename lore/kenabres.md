# Kenabres

The city at the heart of the campaign. A frontier crusader city on the eastern edge of the Worldwound, until recently protected by the wardstone at the Kite.

---

## Status (as of Session 4, end)

**The party has reached the surface.** Kenabres is **devastated.** What they walked out into:

- **The Worldwound has expanded its borders** — the wound now envelops Kenabres itself. The air carries the abyssal sweet-sick smell beneath the smoke. Fat black demonic carrion-birds wheel overhead. Clouds the wrong shape. Light the wrong color.
- **The Cathedral of Saint Clydwell is destroyed** — only a column of smoke marks where it stood.
- **The Kite is gone** — the wardstone fortress reduced to a jagged absence in the western skyline.
- **Population devastated** — from approximately 12,330 pre-rift to perhaps 3,400 survivors. Fled, taken, or dead. The streets are emptier than streets have any right to be.
- **Demonic incursions ongoing** throughout the city — lesser demons crawling walls, quasits controlling tormented innocents at barricades, large demons leaving paths of destruction the size of barns.
- **Refugees barricaded** in scattered groups, exhausted, desperate, vulnerable to quasit-control.
- **Crusader madness** beginning to set in — corrupted Sarenrae knights attempting child-sacrifice for the demon-slayer-blade folk-legend. Whether this is isolated or a pattern is not yet known.
- **The Templars' Nyserian Manor safe house was destroyed by a demon's path** — confirmed in Chapter 4. One of three safe houses off the list.
- **The Blackwing Library was destroyed** — Aravashnial grieved deeply (and for more reasons than the books; see characters/npcs.md Aravashnial SECRET note).
- **Irabeth is alive** — Watch leader (and Anevia's wife) left a note at home: *"Meet at the Defender's Heart."* The party's next destination.

**The party is currently split.** The strike team (Korroc, Thane, Caleth, Anevia, Aravashnial) and the four rescued librarians (at least one of them a fellow Riftwarden, recognized quietly by Aravashnial) are still in the standing hall of the destroyed Blackwing Library after the fight that nearly killed Caleth. The non-combat group (Horgus, Klarah the orphan child, Nageru) is back at **Gwerm Manor** (south of the cathedral; Horgus's intact home). The two groups have not yet reunited — that reconnection is a Session 5 beat.

**Lord Hulrun's fate is still unknown.**

## Geography

**Location:** Eastern Mendev, on the frontier of the Worldwound. Built on the banks of the West Sellen River.

**Major districts and landmarks (pre-attack):**

### The Kite
The fortress that housed the **wardstone** — the magical anchor that prevented demons from crossing into the city's region.

**Status:** **Destroyed** at noon on Armasse, by the Storm King's attack or a coordinated Templar action (the S. V. letter implies both).

### The Cathedral of Saint Clydwell
The great cathedral on the eastern side of Clydwell Plaza. Houses the major religious activities of the city, primarily Iomedaean.

**Status:** Severely damaged. Terendelev's body fell across its façade when Khorramzadeh killed her. Statues toppled from their niches. The great bell tolled once on its broken mountings and went silent. The cathedral may or may not still stand — the party did not see what came next.

### Clydwell Plaza
The broad public square between the Cathedral and the western city. The site of Armasse's main festivities. The location where the rift opened beneath the party's feet.

**Status:** Wracked by the earthquake, demon-summoning, and ongoing demonic incursion at the moment the party fell. Unknown current state.

### The Smith's Quarter
Where Korroc and Thane work. A district of forges, leatherworks, and crafters' shops. Located somewhere south of the cathedral.

**Status:** Unknown.

### The Three Templar Safe Houses
1. **Nyserian Manor** — A noble residence, location unknown
2. **Topaz Solutions** — A merchant business, location unknown
3. **The Tower of Estrod** — A tower-structure, location unknown

(See items.md for details.)

### Horgus's Holdings
Horgus Gwerm owns "half the warehouses south of the cathedral" and is rumored to own the magistrates as well. His properties are major Kenabres commercial real estate.

### The Kenabres Museum
A museum that held **Yaniel's sword Radiance** until approximately two months before the campaign began, when it was stolen by Templars in a heist that was never solved. The Templars who stole it were also Templars infiltrating the museum's own security, per Thane's investigation.

## Politics (Pre-Attack)

### Lord Hulrun
**Title:** Ruler of Kenabres.

**Background:** Aged inquisitor. Has held the city for years. Hardline against demonic influence; sometimes accused of being *too* hardline (some of his witch-hunts have caught innocents).

**Status:** Was on the cathedral stage at the moment of the attack. **Fate unknown.** If he survived, he's a major political figure for the party to deal with. If he died, the city is currently without its central authority.

### The Mendevian Crusade Command
A network of crusader marshals, paladins, and military officers running the war effort from Kenabres. Operates somewhat separately from the city's civic government.

**Likely contacts on the surface:**
- **Irabeth** (named by Anevia as their first contact)
- The Eagle Watch leadership

## Festivals and Holidays

### Armasse
**Date:** 16 Arodus.

**Original purpose:** A scholarly holiday for priests and historians to study the lessons of past wars.

**Modern purpose:** Since Aroden's death, transformed into a public festival focused on:
- Training commoners in weaponry
- Choosing squires
- Ordaining new priests
- Jousting competitions
- Mock duels
- Battle reenactments
- General feasting and festivity

**Plot significance:** The day the wardstone fell. The day Terendelev died. The day the campaign opened.

## The Underdark Beneath Kenabres

Beneath the city lies an extensive network of natural caverns, old sewers and cisterns, and inhabited spaces. The party has been navigating this since the rift opened beneath Clydwell Plaza.

### Layers (as the party has experienced them):

**Upper Layer:**
- Crusader camps and tunnels (long-abandoned from the First Crusade era)
- The shrine of Torag (now reconsecrated)
- Cavern of carved statues (First Crusade dead, made into portraits by the touched)

**Middle Layer:**
- Nathholm — the mongrelfolk lake-village
- Various smaller passages and dead-ends
- The Vescavor dens

**Lower Sewer Layer:**
- Pre-Worldwound Kenabres sewer system
- The maze of the cistern (built by Templars/Vescavor for demon-summoning concealment)
- Hosilla's ritual chamber

**Surface Access:**
- Multiple chimneys and shafts to surface buildings (one of which the party will soon climb)
- The old sewer outflow corridor (the party will likely emerge somewhere along the river or in a sewer-access building)

### Inhabitants:
- **Nathholm mongrelfolk** (allied)
- **Vescavor mongrelfolk** (hostile, largely defeated in Session 3)
- **Various beasts:** giant lizards, rats, cockroaches, snakes, carrion flies, maggots
- **Other tribes** — Lann mentioned them but they have not yet been encountered
- **Templar operatives** — actively moving through the underdark, supplying the Vescavor

## Cultural Background

### Faiths Practiced in Kenabres
- **Iomedae** — primary state faith, inheritor of Aroden's mantle, patron of the crusade
- **Torag** — common among the dwarven population; Korroc's faith
- **Shelyn** — present in the artistic and healing communities; Caleth's faith
- **Erastil** — common among the rural population around Kenabres
- **Cayden Cailean** — popular in the soldiery and taverns
- **Alseta** — minor goddess of doors, thresholds, transitions, and the new year. Small but real presence in Kenabres among doorkeepers, locksmiths, year-end celebrants, and a handful of inquisitor families. **Thane's family faith** — his mother served her quietly and passed her on the same way. Treated as a respected minor deity by the surface church; no friction with the crusade. (See [characters/thane.md](../characters/thane.md) Faith section for the load-bearing canon.)
- Various others — Kenabres is a crusader-cosmopolitan city

### Demographics
- **Humans** dominate (Mendev is historically a human kingdom)
- **Dwarves** are a meaningful minority — many came south after the loss of Khar-Zadûn and have settled in the trade quarters
- **Half-elves** less common but present
- **Aasimar and other planar-touched** are particularly drawn to the crusader life; not rare
- **Halflings, gnomes, half-orcs** all present in smaller numbers

### Atmosphere
**Pre-attack:** A weary city. Decades of crusader warfare have ground morale low. Armasse was an essential pressure-release valve. Smiles were rare; furrowed brows were the default expression on the street. People drank too much. The young trained too hard.

**Now:** Unknown. The party will discover it.

## The Wardstones

A magical network of stones that prevent demonic incursion across the Worldwound border. Each wardstone is keyed to a region; the destruction of one weakens the network as a whole.

**The Kenabres wardstone** was housed in the Kite. Its destruction:
- Allowed Khorramzadeh to enter the city directly
- Allowed lesser demons to manifest through the resulting rift
- Has unknown effects on the larger wardstone network — likely cascading weaknesses across the border
- May trigger a major demonic offensive on a wider front

**Plot relevance:** Replacing or restoring wardstones may become a major campaign objective. The Templar plot to destroy them is now confirmed to be coordinated.

## Probable Surface State (When Party Arrives)

The party should expect, when they finally surface:

- **Significant physical damage** — many buildings down, fires, ruined streets
- **Demon incursions ongoing** — though probably suppressed by surviving crusaders
- **Refugees** — many people displaced, many wounded
- **Martial law** — whoever is in charge will likely have imposed strict controls
- **Misinformation and paranoia** — rumors flying about what happened
- **Active enemy presence** — Templars still operating, possibly desperate to cover tracks given the loss of Hosilla
- **Allies looking for them** — Irabeth, the Eagle Watch, possibly Caleth's Spire handlers
- **Need for the letter** — whoever is in charge needs to know what the party knows, urgently

The party arriving at the surface with the S. V. letter, Radiance, the Baphomet symbols, Sull's pledge of mongrelfolk alliance, and four mythic-touched silver scales is going to be a *moment* for the surface authorities. Treat it with appropriate weight when it happens.
