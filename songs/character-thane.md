🗝️ "Read the Door" — Dark Cinematic Trap-Rap Character Anthem

Standalone CHARACTER ANTHEM for **Thane**, the dwarf Knife Master rogue / inquisitor of Alseta — the doorkeeper. Not a per-session song and deliberately NOT the project's other rap (the goofy boom-bap "Skinsuit" diss). This one is dark, serious, cinematic, and personal: the watcher who decides who has business inside the door and who does not.

This is now a **proper, full-length rap** — no longer trimmed to a (mistaken) 1,000-character lyric cap. Full structure: Intro → Verse 1 → Hook → Verse 2 (the double-time chopper) → Hook → Verse 3 → Bridge → Hook (final) → Outro. The **hook recurs three times** like a real rap hook, and the verses develop more than one angle: the doorkeeper/trust creed (V1), the trespasser fury and the bright-loud-problem knife-work (the chopper), and the banked-flame grief and the hammer-and-hinge bond with his cousin (V3).

The spine is **trust** — but Thane's trust is the doorkeeper's discernment. He doesn't trust; he *verifies, reads, checks.* His mother's lockpicking mantra is his creed ("Listen first — hinges and bolts speak. Look second — marks at the keyhole. Touch third — the plate tells you if the lock's been forced"), so the whole lyric thinks in door grammar: latch, hinge, bolt, plate, frame, breach, credential, "door closed proper." Demons and cultists are *trespassers* — false papers at the gate. Under all of it burns **the banked flame**, his father's death carried as fuel, not a wound — a small clean fire he feeds one mark at a time. The twin daggers live in the fighting without explanation: mother's blade leading, father's wedding-knife reversed in the off-hand, both ways at once. And "no Stonevein has ever sheathed a wet blade" — he wipes the steel after every kill.

**Verse 1 reads spacious; Verse 2 is a deliberately dense wall of short syllables and rapid internal rhyme** — written so the only way to fit the words to the beat is to rap them fast. It carries a short bracketed `[Verse - double time]` cue because Suno responds to those.

Sound: dark, aggressive MODERN trap-rap — brooding minor-key piano, heavy distorted 808 sub, rapid triplet hi-hat rolls, tense cinematic strings. Controlled fury, not chaos. The chopper is a tempo/flow shift WITHIN the same dark-rap register, not a genre change. Rapped throughout — no sung hook, no autotune, no profanity. ## Troubleshooting

1. **If the double-time / chopper STILL doesn't trigger:** This is the known failure. Fix it from BOTH ends at once. (a) Keep/strengthen the bracket tag — `[Verse - double time, chopper, breathless]`. (b) Push the LYRIC density even harder — the words themselves force the speed: strip remaining connectors so the bars are pure short stabs (`latch draft glass pass, through back breach gap`), more syllables per bar than Verse 1 can possibly hold at normal speed. (c) Raise the stated tempo — try `150-160 BPM`; a low BPM with "double-time feel" is exactly what rendered as merely slow last time. The contrast must be Verse 1 spacious / the chopper a wall. If it still raps at Verse 1 speed, it's not dense enough yet — cram more.
2. **If Suno SINGS the hook instead of rapping it:** Front-load `dark rapped vocal, spoken rhythmic delivery, rapped hook NOT sung, no melody, no autotune`. Tag the hook `[Hook - rapped, dark, aggressive, no singing]`. Any melodic crooning, regenerate.
3. **If it drifts to boom-bap / rock / pop instead of dark modern trap-rap:** Push `modern trap-rap, 808 sub-bass, trap hi-hats, cinematic piano and strings, NO boom-bap, NO live drums, NO guitar, NO pop`. This project's recurring Suno failure is guitar/twang/rock — if any shows up, regenerate immediately. Keep it 808s and piano, not a band.
4. **If the flow drags off the beat or mumbles:** Add `tight pocket, syllables locked to the trap drums, crisp enunciation`. The door-grammar lists ("read the latch, clock the latch, mark the gap in the gate") need to snap percussively on the hi-hats. If the vocal lags the beat, regenerate.
5. **If the rhymes don't land:** The internal rhymes are load-bearing — "the gate / awake," "I'd wait / the break," "the watch / the plan." If Suno glides over them, emphasize `dense multi-syllabic internal rhyme, punchy consonants`.
6. **If profanity or ad-libs creep in:** Add `clean, no profanity, no explicit ad-libs`. This anthem stays clean by design — controlled fury, not gutter-mouth.
