⚔️ "When the Sky Fell on Kenabres" — An Epic Ballad
A four-voice march for the Wardstone Four, sung in the dark beneath a broken city.

🎵 LYRICS
[Intro — low choir, distant bell, slow forge-hammer pulse]
Aroden is dust. The wardstone is dust.
Eight hundred miles of fire and rust.
A dragon fell. A city burns.
And down in the dark, a hammer turns…

[Verse 1 — Korroc, dwarf-paladin baritone, deep and steady]
I was born to the bellows, baptized in flame,
My father's anvil, my mother's name,
I forged my oath where the iron sang —
Now the sky has cracked, and the great bells rang.
She fell from the sun, the silver one,
And her last grace caught me as I came down,
A scale at my belt and a hammer in hand —
Torag still walks in the under-land.
[Pre-chorus — building, drums entering]
Down where the lightless rivers run,
Down where the wardstone's work undone,
We rose, we four, from the rubble and ash —
Ironbound, oathbound, sworn to the last.

[Chorus — full four-part, anthemic, soaring]
🔥 We are the hammer! We are the blade!
We are the prayer the silver scale made!
Burn down the gate, let the dark run red —
The city above is not yet dead!
🔥 Anvil and dagger, glaive and fist —
Four hands raised in the under-mist —
Rise from the rift where the dragon bled —
The city above is not yet dead!

[Verse 2 — Thane, dwarf-rogue/inquisitor, gravelly, half-spoken, Eagle Watch fierce]
I lost my brothers to the Worldwound years,
I drowned my grieving, I drank my fears,
I learned the alleys, I learned the knife —
A blade in the shadow can still fight for life.
I am no skulker, I am no thief,
I am the scalpel that cuts grief,
The Watch on the wall when the wall is gone —
I'll stab the dark until the dawn.

[Verse 3 — Caleth, half-elf paladin/wizard of Shelyn, clean lyric tenor, eldritch shimmer]
From the Mordant Spire where the sea-tongues hum,
Where the elder-eldest songs are sung,
They sent me out with a hidden name,
A glaive of leaf and a heart of flame.
Shelyn taught me that beauty has teeth,
That a love-song hides a sword in its sheath,
That mercy cuts truer than any spite —
I am art with edges. I am light.

[Verse 4 — Nageru, aasimar monk/paladin, smooth low tenor, almost whispered, then thunder]
I came to your gates a stranger born,
Wind in my robe and a sky still warm,
I carry no steel and I wear no plate —
The thunder is patient. The thunder will wait.
My blood remembers the host above,
My bones are tuned to a quiet love,
I open my hand and the heavens speak —
Stillness breaks what the strong call weak.

[Bridge — drums drop, single voice, then build]
And the silver dragon, falling, falling, gave us her last word:
Live. Fight. Carry them home.
Live. Fight. Carry them home.
Live. FIGHT. CARRY THEM HOME —
[Drums explode back in, double-time, war-march]
🔥 We have walked through the maggot-dark,
🔥 We have lit a god's forgotten spark,
🔥 We have set our shields on a fallen shrine —
🔥 And the Storm King's hour will be undone in time!

[Final Chorus — bigger, all four voices layered, choir behind]
⚔️ We are the hammer! We are the blade!
We are the prayer the silver scale made!
Burn down the gate, let the dark run red —
The city above is not yet dead!
⚔️ Forge-son, knife-son, spire-son, sky-son —
Four became one when the wardstone was undone —
Rise from the rift where the dragon bled —
The city above is NOT. YET. DEAD.

[Outro — slow, the forge-pulse returns, fades]
A hammer turns in the under-land…
A scale of silver in every hand…
Above us a city, below us a vow —
Mythic walks the under-dark now.

Four-voice arrangement: a deep gravelly dwarven baritone for the lead, a rougher half-spoken bass for the rogue verse, a clean ringing eldritch tenor for the wizard verse, a smooth low half-whispered tenor that erupts into thunder for the monk verse. Massive layered four-part choir on every chorus. Tempo 88 BPM verses, 132 BPM choruses. Dynamic range from near-whisper bridge to wall-of-sound finale. Think Blind Guardian meets Heilung meets Two Steps From Hell, with a Pathfinder/Warhammer fantasy-crusader atmosphere. Cinematic, grim, defiant, holy.
