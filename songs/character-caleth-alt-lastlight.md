## Style (paste into Suno style box)

Crusader synthwave — a single cohesive synth-grunge hybrid texture, present THROUGHOUT, no genre swap. Atmospheric cinematic synth pads and arpeggios, warm retro analog synth leads, and thick crashing distorted grunge guitars all coexisting at once, the whole song. Military snare-driven percussion, mid-tempo around 95 BPM, steady and driving. Low, introspective, weathered male vocal — emotional, melancholy, restrained, not clean pop, not a belter. Dynamic swells WITHIN the one register: verses quieter and intimate with synth and muted guitar; choruses bigger and anthemic with full distorted guitar wall and wider pads — but never leaving the hybrid sound, never a pure-synth verse becoming a metal chorus. Mood: rain on stone, dawn breaking over a wounded world, hope and exhaustion braided together. No tempo changes, no key changes, single hybrid register start to finish. No party/neon brightness, no club beat. No instrumental padding between sections; vocals carry. Final line near-spoken, low and tired.

## Troubleshooting

1. **If Suno drops the grunge guitars and goes pure synthwave:** Lead the style box with "Heavy distorted grunge guitars present in every section, layered with synth — not synthwave alone" and add "thick crashing guitar wall under the chorus." The guitar weight is half the character; it cannot be optional.
2. **If it goes too "party/neon" synthwave:** Cut any brightness — emphasize "dark, melancholy, weathered crusader synthwave, no club beat, no neon shimmer, mid-tempo and heavy." Push "rain on stone" and "cinematic" to the front.
3. **If Suno tries to swap genres (quiet synth verse → metal chorus):** This is the failure mode to guard hardest. Re-state "ONE hybrid synth-grunge texture throughout, dynamic swells only, NO genre change between sections." Make the verses "intimate but still hybrid," not synth-only.
4. **If the vocal comes back too clean / pop / female:** Add "low introspective weathered solo male vocal, grunge-rock grit, not polished, not pop" to the front. He should sound tired and human, carrying something.
5. **The outro "Just enough light. I keep walking."** should land near-spoken and low. If Suno sings it melodically, add (spoken) after it in the lyrics box.
