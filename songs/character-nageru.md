⚔️ "The Thunder Wakes" — Martial Death Metal

Standalone CHARACTER ANTHEM for **Nageru**, the Archon-blooded aasimar Scaled Fist monk / Oath of Vengeance paladin — and the deliberate MIRROR and ANSWER to his session-5 meditation, [songs/session5.md](session5.md) ("The Thunder Turns Over in Its Sleep"), the near-whispered shakuhachi piece built so the thunder deliberately never woke. This is the thunder finally WAKING: the Oath-of-Vengeance half of him arriving in full. The session-5 refrain is inverted here — *the thunder is patient / the thunder will wait / turns over in its sleep* becomes *the thunder is awake / the thunder will not wait / and does not sleep.* The critical craft move: the MUSIC is a relentless death-metal war-march start to finish (single register), but the STILLNESS lives in the WORDS. The lyrics stay cold, measured, liturgical, disciplined — Irori's order and a monk's calm delivered over the storm. This is wrath that was *decided*, not felt: "Restoration, not rage. Not rage — it is tidying." That tension between the cold words and the relentless music is the entire point. ## Style (paste into Suno style box)

Martial death metal, a Viking war-march. Fast galloping rhythm around 170 BPM, driving double-kick drums, palm-muted gallop riffs, soaring twin-guitar harmonies and triumphant lead melodies over the top. Harsh but fully INTELLIGIBLE mid-range death growl on the verses — the words must be clearly heard, every line understood, controlled and precise, not a chaotic gurgle. Huge anthemic gang-shout chorus that is genuinely SINGABLE — a crowd of voices roaring in unison, clear and clean enough to chant along. Triumphant, martial, disciplined, holy battle-metal: a paladin's controlled fury, not demonic, not satanic, not brutal-death, not chaotic. Single register throughout — relentless war-march start to finish, no quiet flute intro, no meditative section, no genre swap. Dynamic swells inside the genre only: heavier verses, soaring choruses. Bridge is a massive gang-shout climax, same genre. Epic, ordered wrath. Male vocals only.

## Troubleshooting

1. **If the growl goes UNINTELLIGIBLE (the worst failure — the liturgical words MUST be heard):** This is the one that kills the song. The entire concept is cold, measured, ORDERED words delivered over the storm; if you can't make out the lyrics, it's just noise and Nageru is gone. Lead the style box with "harsh but fully intelligible mid-range death growl, every word clearly understood, controlled and precise." If it persists, drop to a cleaner "raspy shouted half-growl" or tag the verses as needing clarity. Regenerate until you can hear "Restoration, not rage."
2. **If it goes chaotic / satanic / brutal-death instead of martial / holy:** Add "martial death metal, triumphant Viking war-march, soaring twin-guitar harmonies, NOT brutal death, NOT satanic, NOT chaotic, NOT blast-beat grindcore." This is a holy warrior's disciplined wrath — it should sound epic and ordered, like an army marching in step, not like a pit collapsing.
3. **If it loses the clean singable GANG-SHOUT chorus:** The chorus is the payoff — "Evil must die" has to land as a crowd roaring in unison, clear enough to chant. Add "huge clean gang-shout chorus, layered male voices in unison, anthemic and singable, NOT growled." The verses growl; the chorus is shouted clean and big.
4. **If Suno tries to insert a quiet / meditative section (reject this):** Single register only. The stillness is in the WORDS, not the arrangement — there is no quiet flute intro, no meditation break, no soft passage that detonates into metal (Suno chokes on that swap, and it's wrong for this song anyway). If a quiet intro or breakdown appears, regenerate and add "no quiet intro, no acoustic break, relentless metal start to finish, single register."
5. **Tempo note:** Fast gallop, around 170 BPM — driving and relentless, but still a war-march you go to battle to, not a chaotic blast. The word "melodic" was removed because it pulled Suno too soft; "martial death metal" at 170 hits the disciplined-but-savage target. If it ever loses the deliberate, ORDERED feel, tag "galloping war-march, 170 BPM, driving but controlled, NOT blast-beat."
6. **The bridge is the answer to session 5.** "The thunder is awake / The thunder will not wait / and the thunder does not sleep" is the deliberate inversion of the meditation's refrain — the patient thunder finally waking. Let it be the biggest gang-shout moment in the song. "These also" closes it the way the meditation did, but now it's a war-cry, not a whisper.
