Session 11 song for [chapters/11-the-latch-that-held.md](../chapters/11-the-latch-that-held.md) — the eve of the assault on Drezen, Thane's POV. The night the Queen's satchel opens and a seventy-year-old letter, signed first in four Stonevein hands, puts his fallen father's reconstructed map of the citadel into his own palm. Not a session recap: this is interior state only. The song is built on the letter's closing line — *"stone remembers, and so do dwarves."* Its whole argument is Thane's argument: **the grief and the gift are the same thing, and he had only ever seen the one side of the stone.** Verse 1 is what he thought he inherited (a scrap of handwriting, a badge of sky-steel, an empty grave, a banked flame). Verse 2 is what was actually done in the dark seventy years ago (twelve hands rebuilding a lost fortress from memory). The bridge is the key crossing seventy years. Verse 3 is the oath — walking through the door his father drew, his cousin reading the same doubled grief beside him. Name-free by project pattern (no "Thane," "Drezen," "Galfrey"); "Stonevein" is held back and the door/threshold imagery carries his quiet Alsetan register without ever naming the goddess.

Single register start to finish — a funeral march that becomes a vow, building in layers and never in genre. Paste the LYRICS block into Suno's lyrics box, the STYLE block into the style box, and "Stone Remembers" into the title field.

## Troubleshooting

1. **If it drifts to rock / guitar / twang (this project's #1 Suno failure):** Regenerate immediately. Reinforce `NO guitar, NO electric guitar, NO rock drums, all-acoustic stone and voice, hammer-on-anvil percussion and low drone only`. Keep it stone, drum, drone, and voices — never a band. If a guitar or twang shows up at all, discard the take.
2. **If the choir never triggers on the refrain:** The refrain must sound bigger than the verses by voices alone. Push `massed male choir enters in unison on the refrain, verses are one solo voice`. You can also cue it directly in the tag — `[Refrain - full male choir, unison]` — while keeping the verse tags bare so the contrast reads. If the whole song is choir front-to-back, strengthen the solo framing on the verses instead.
3. **If it drifts into power-metal / anthemic-rock / a triumphant major-key lift:** This is the most likely register failure — the "oath" energy tempts Suno toward stadium metal. Hold it down with `funeral-march tempo, minor/modal, somber and heavy, NO power metal, NO uplifting major-key swell, builds in weight not brightness`. The climax should get *heavier and lower*, not faster or shinier.
4. **If the tempo speeds up as it builds:** Restate `64 BPM throughout, unhurried, tempo never changes — build only in layers`. The song must gain mass, not speed. A quickening is a failed take.
5. **If the vocal comes out too high / thin / female-tinged:** Front-load `deep solo male voice, dwarf bass-baritone, low and grave, no female vocals`. Regenerate if the lead sits in a tenor or higher.
6. **If it sings the spoken intro/outro as full melody:** Those bracketed stage directions and the short closing lines are meant to be near-spoken over the drone. Tag them `[Intro - spoken, low]` / `[Outro - spoken, fading]` if Suno over-melodizes them.
7. **If the ending fades or noodles with instrumental padding:** Ask for `clean heavy ending, resolve on the last low voice, no instrumental tail` so the final "So do we" lands and stops.
