🔥 "The Smith Made of Light" — Funeral-Doom Hymn Version

A short, single-register hymn for one moment of [chapters/04-the-smith-in-the-ruin.md](../chapters/04-the-smith-in-the-ruin.md) — Korroc kneeling over Caleth's body in the Blackwing Library, asking his god for a fire he has not earned, and being given it. No chapter recap, no four-stage build, no power-metal climax. Sustained funeral-doom hymn throughout. ~2 minutes. Paste this whole block straight into Suno's lyrics box.

🎵 LYRICS (Suno-Ready)

[Intro - slow hand drums at 60 BPM, distant anvil ring, deep low male choir drone, no melody]

The half-elf falls.
The mace comes down.

[Verse 1 - low gravelly baritone, chanted half-spoken, choir drone holding underneath]

I am only a smith.
I am only a smith.
My hammer is hammer.
My hand is hand.
The half-elf is dead at my knees.

[Refrain - choir opens a fraction, voice steady, no lift]

Torag. Torag.
The Smith made me a tool.
Now the tool must be more.

[Verse 2 - same register, anvil ring under, no melodic shift]

I lay my hands on his chest.
I have no spell left in me.
I have no breath of my own.
I ask the Smith for a fire I have not earned.

[Refrain - same register]

Torag. Torag.
The Smith made me a tool.
Now the tool must be more.

[Verse 3 - the transformation, still chanted, choir slightly fuller, no power-metal lift]

The fire comes.
Through the wrists.
Through the chest.
Through the place a smith keeps his name.

I am no longer a smith.
I am the fire.
And the fire is the breath.
And the breath is the brother.

[Closing refrain - choir holds drone, voice low, spoken-chanted]

Torag. Torag.
The tool was more.
The tool was more.
For one moment, the tool was light.

[Outro - three slow anvil rings, choir fades, deep cavern reverb, silence]

[Spoken, almost whispered, final line]

The half-elf breathed again.

🎚️ SUNO STYLE BOX

Slow funeral-doom hymn. Hand drums and distant anvil ring at 60 BPM. Deep low male choir holds a sustained drone throughout. Lead: gravelly dwarven baritone, chanted half-spoken, weathered, NEVER sung at full melody. Each line a hammer-strike — short, heavy, deliberate. NO bridge, NO chorus lift, NO genre shifts, NO climax, NO orchestral build. Only voice, hand drums, anvil, and choir drone. Warm stone-cavern reverb on vocals, organic acoustic, NO synths, NO strings, NO brass, NO guitars. Wardruna meets a slow Latin funeral mass. Anti-melody — chanted, not sung.

~640 characters. Comfortable headroom.

🎯 If the First Generation Misses

**If Suno tries to sing rather than chant:** Add `[Verse 1 - SPOKEN-CHANTED, gravelly half-speech, NOT sung, monks-at-vespers register]` for every verse tag. Suno responds to the "spoken-chanted" cue but sometimes needs it repeated.

**If Suno drifts toward melody or pop in the middle:** Regenerate. The most common failure is Suno inventing a melodic hook around the *"Torag, Torag"* refrain. If it does that, add `[Refrain - monotone chant, no melodic interval, anvil ring under]` and try again. The refrain must be flat, not sung.

**If the choir gets too churchy / too Catholic / too soaring:** Try `low male monastic vocal pad, Norse rather than Latin, throat-singing texture, ground tone only — no harmony lift`.

**If the song generates too long or feels padded:** It's about 2 minutes; if Suno stretches it past 3, regenerate. Funeral hymns don't pad.

**The closing whispered line is the gut-punch.** If Suno fades before it lands or rushes through it, regenerate. Tag it as `[Final line - lone whispered voice, all other sound CUT, deep cavern reverb tail]`.

Generate two or three takes. The right one will feel like a Wardruna track that happens to be in English, not like a song. If it feels like a song, it isn't the take.

🎯 If You Want Variations

**Even shorter (~90 second cut):** Drop Verse 2 entirely. The song becomes: intro → V1 → refrain → V3 → closing refrain → outro. Loses the "asking" step but the transformation still lands.

**Spoken-word / poem version:** Strip the music entirely. Speak the lyrics over a single sustained low organ drone and three anvil rings. 60-90 seconds. Closer to liturgy than song.
