## Style (paste into Suno style box)

Driving, propulsive crusader synthwave — bright and anthemic, but aching underneath, NOT a party track. One cohesive synthwave register THROUGHOUT, no genre swap. Steady four-on-the-floor pulse, relentless and forward-moving. Glittering arpeggiated synths cycling under everything, big warm analog synth leads, lush wide pads, gated-reverb drums hitting hard and nostalgic. Emotional soaring solo male vocal — intimate and a little weary in the verses, then lifting open and bright on the chorus, some head-voice / near-falsetto reach welcome on "Radiance" and "carry me." Wide neon-melancholy atmosphere: hope and cost braided together, a current of sadness running under the brightness. Dynamic swells WITHIN the one register — verses quieter and pulsing, pre-chorus building, chorus blooming huge and luminous, bridge stripped to arpeggio and vocal before the final lift — but never leaving synthwave, never a soft-synth verse becoming a different genre. Cinematic, holy-but-modern, propulsive but tender. No tempo changes, no key changes. No grunge guitar wall, no metal. No instrumental padding between sections; the pulse and vocal carry. Final line near-spoken, soft and certain over a last shimmer.

## Troubleshooting

1. **If Suno makes it too cheerful / club / party-neon:** This is the main failure mode for a bright synthwave. Lead the style box with "melancholy crusader synthwave, aching and bittersweet under the brightness, NOT happy, NOT a club track" and add "minor-key lift, hope braided with grief." The propulsion stays; the cheer must not.
2. **If it drops the melancholy and goes empty/glossy:** Push "weathered, emotional, a current of sadness under the neon glow" and "the brightness costs something." Keep "neon-melancholy" near the front. The song should soar AND hurt.
3. **If the vocal comes back too thin / weak on the lift:** Add "powerful emotional solo male vocal that genuinely soars into the chorus, full and open, head-voice reach, not breathy or thin." The chorus on "Radiance" is the emotional peak — it must actually lift, not stay flat.
4. **If Suno tries to swap genres (soft verse → heavy/rock chorus):** Re-state "ONE synthwave register start to finish, dynamic swells only, NO genre change, NO guitars." Make verses "quieter synthwave," not a different instrument set.
5. **If the four-on-the-floor pulse disappears or the arpeggio becomes a sustained pad:** Push "steady driving four-on-the-floor kick throughout, cycling arpeggiated synth hook as the spine, NOT a slow ballad, NOT a sustained pad." The relentless forward motion is what separates this from Option A.
6. **The outro "It found my hand. It keeps finding mine."** should land soft and near-spoken over a final shimmer. If Suno belts it, add (spoken) after it in the lyrics box.
