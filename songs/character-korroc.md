## Troubleshooting

1. **If the drum solo is buried or doesn't appear:** This is the hardest thing to get out of Suno — expect several regenerations. Make the `[Drum solo]` tags longer and more insistent (`[Drum solo — long, unaccompanied, kit only, no guitar, no vocals]`), and push "drums louder than everything, guitars drop out under the solo" in the style box. The "band drops back" framing gets a drum-FORWARD break more reliably than a fully naked solo. For more Peart, add "odd-time fills, 7/8 and 5/4 accents" — but that fights the no-tempo-change line, so only if you want it proggier.
2. **If Suno makes it symphonic / power-metal / soaring:** Lead the style box with "Raw downtuned groove metal, no orchestra, no choir, no soaring." The grit, the groove, and the drums are the point — keep it grounded.
3. **If the vocal comes back clean / pop / female:** Add "gruff solo male baritone, gravel grit, spoken-edge delivery" to the front. To push the guttural dwarven war-chant harder, add "deep guttural growl, throaty, from the chest" — but keep the words intelligible so the chorus still shouts along; a full death-growl buries the lyrics.
4. **If the groove loses its drive:** Hold "around 100 BPM, driving and relentless, stomping." This is a forward-pushing war-march, not a slow plod — but don't let it gallop away from the stomp either.
5. **If a choir or cathedral pad creeps in:** Add "solo male vocal only, no backing choir, no church organ." Korroc's faith is a forge, not a cathedral — metal and stone, not stained glass.
6. **The outro "No. The anvil holds."** should land near-spoken and flat — the calm of a smith rejecting a bad weld. If Suno sings it melodically, add (spoken) after it in the lyrics box.
