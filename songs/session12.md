Title: Half Your Wounds

[Intro]
(a hammer keeps the time)
Hold still, now.
This'll hurt me more than you.

[Verse 1]
The hammer falls, the anvil holds,
the blade comes singing from the coal.
It never counts the blows it's spared —
I keep that ledger whole.
I was built low, I was built wide,
set square upon the smithy floor.
So bring the weight you cannot lift.
That's what an anvil's for.

[Refrain]

Set the load on older bone.
Give me half! (give me half)
No one bears the fire alone.
Don't you spend what you can't bleed,

I'll be taking half your wounds now.
Swing away. I'll hold. I'll mend.

and I say a prayer and carry it.
I can't be always at your shoulder,
can't be first through every door,
so I take the wound the long way home.
That's what an anvil's for.

[Verse 3]
You'll catch me pouring gold on me
and think the mercy's flowing wrong.
But I'm holding blood that isn't mine,
and the wall has to stay strong.
A crack runs where the strain sits down,
so I don't sit down no more.
I keep the keeper fit to keep.
That's what an anvil's for.

[Bridge - half-time, low]
My fathers' hands are in that stone,
out west, where no bell rings.
They bore their half and then the rest —
and a wall recalls such things.

one span, one hill, no more.
