## Style (paste into Suno style box)

Character count: 994 (spaces included — under the 1,000 cap)

Gospel-soul with emotional grandeur. Piano-forward — a piano-man's playing leads the whole song, intimate then thunderous. Soulful baritone lead that begins near-spoken and tender, vulnerable, then opens up and SOARS on every chorus — full-throated, belting from the chest, reaching for the top of his range. Hammond organ swells, walking bass, brushed-then-driving drums, tambourine, hand-claps on two and four. Strong call-and-response between the lead and a full gospel choir answering "yes." Verses stay intimate and confessional; choruses lift to soaring, cinematic, anthemic height with the choir swelling huge behind the belt. Single warm register, never changing genre — gospel and classic soul throughout. Preacher's cadence, sincere, never ironic. Major key, mid-tempo with a swaying lift that climbs. Choir grows from a few voices to a packed room. Live-room warmth, analog and human. No rock distortion, no EDM, no pop gloss.

## Troubleshooting

1. **If the choruses stay flat/contained:** The `[Chorus - soaring, open chest]` tag plus the "oh"/"ohh" pickups are the soar levers. If Suno still holds back, change the tag to `[Chorus - belting, full power]` and stretch the peak vowels ("ohhh," "naaame") to force a sustain.

2. **If the piano isn't prominent enough:** The style box front-loads "piano-forward" and "piano-man's playing leads." If organ or drums dominate, regenerate, or temporarily move "piano leads the whole song" into the very first sentence and trim a later instrument.

3. **If it drifts toward pop/rock on the big moments:** Keep "No rock distortion, no EDM, no pop gloss" as the final line. If it still drifts, add "stay in church" inside the chorus tag.

4. **If verse/chorus dynamics are too even:** The intimate-verse / soaring-chorus contrast is what makes it fly. Add `[Verse - intimate, piano only]` tags so the lift into the chorus has somewhere to climb from.

5. **If the final chorus doesn't peak above the others:** The extra third "Yes. (Oh, yes.)" line and `[full congregation, peak, belting]` tag mark it as the summit. If it sounds the same as earlier choruses, add "biggest moment of the song, full choir" inside the tag.
