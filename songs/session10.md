## Option 1: "Carry Her in Candlelight" — Intimate Orchestral Ballad

The private devotion. The man who carries his goddess in silence, cleans her altar, restores her house, and tells no one why. Aching, hushed, restrained.

### Style (paste into Suno style box — 712 chars)

Intimate orchestral ballad, film-score chamber arrangement. Opens on a solo cello, sorrowful and bare, slowly joined by warm strings and soft piano. Male lead vocal — warm baritone, controlled, restrained, never belted; the feeling lives in what is held back. Late-night ballad meets cinematic score intimacy. Single register throughout, no genre shift, no key change. Sparse verses on cello and piano; strings swell gently under the chorus but never erupt. Bridge strips to voice and cello alone. Candlelit, reverent, aching, hushed. Slow tempo, rubato feel. NO drums, NO electric guitar, NO percussion, NO choir belting. Soft dynamic ceiling — this never gets loud. Gentle, sustained ending that fades on cello and a single held string.

## Option 2: "Brush of Fire" — Dark Fusion (v2)

The anger. The controlled man losing control. The calculator who stops calculating and starts burning. Aggressive breakbeat, lean and punchy. The fury IS the faith. All Caleth's metaphors — paint, sketch, frame, angles, the board.

### Style (paste into Suno style box — 698 chars)

Dark fusion, 135 BPM. Soaring bright synthwave arpeggios over punching breakbeat drums — glossy, euphoric synth runs shimmering above gritty big-beat aggression. The contrast IS the sound: neon-bright cascading arpeggios on top, distorted bass stabs and hard breaks underneath. Male vocal, controlled menace building to euphoric roar. Dark pop hook with rave energy. Sidechained pulsing synth pads, filtered sweeping builds into slamming drops. Choruses explode with layered shimmering arpeggios and driving four-on-the-floor. Bridge shifts glassy and crystalline — frost-toned synths, arpeggios go ice-cold. Dangerous, euphoric, cinematic. No orchestral, no metal guitars.
