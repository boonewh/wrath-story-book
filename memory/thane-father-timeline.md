# Thane & Korroc knew their fathers — do not write the sons as never having met them

**Canon (confirmed by the user, July 2026, during the session 11 song passes):**

- Thorek Stonevein (Thane's father) and Borin Stonevein (Korroc's father) died in the **Fourth Mendevian Crusade when their sons were children**. Both sons **knew their fathers** as young dwarves and were raised by them in their early years. Thane's banked-flame grief comes from losing a father he knew and loved — not from an absence he never met.
- The **Drezen blueprints letter** (Chapter 11, *The Latch That Held*) was written **~70 years ago — roughly 20 years BEFORE the sons were born** (Korroc is 51). So: the letter/map predates the sons; the fathers' deaths do not. The chapter's phrasing is "to arm a son not yet born for the fight of his life" — that refers to the *writing of the letter*, not to the fathers' lifespans.
- What Thorek **never lived to see** is the *reclamation of Drezen* — the day the key is used — NOT his son.

**The error this file guards against:** the session 11 song "Stone Remembers" briefly shipped the bridge line "a key in the palm of a son / **he never lived to see**" — implying Thorek never saw Thane. User caught it. Corrected to "a key in the palm of a son, / **a dawn he'd never see**."

**Rule of thumb:** grief-as-inheritance imagery for the Stonevein cousins may say the fathers never saw *the victory / the dawn / the reclaimed citadel / the sons grown into the fight* — but never that they didn't see *the sons*.
