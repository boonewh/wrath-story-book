Chapter 1 ("When the Sky Fell on Kenabres") was written by a previous Claude before the project decided on the Abercrombie POV-by-stakes method or the song-per-POV pattern. The user fed in raw session notes and Opus produced a Korroc-leaning narrative because Korroc was the most narratively grounded character in the raw material. The user then realized: this was accidentally a Korroc chapter, and the rest of the campaign could rotate POV to balance it out. The first song ("When the Sky Fell on Kenabres") was written for Korroc, and the title-doubling is now read as deliberate: the chapter is the song's narrative.

**Why:** Context for any future structural decisions. The project is still evolving — the Abercrombie POV method, song-per-POV, two-line poetic chapter titles, and other patterns all emerged organically, not as a designed system. If a new chapter wants to break a pattern, that's allowed; new patterns can become canon if they earn it.

**How to apply:**
- Do not treat any of the project's structural rules ([[README.md]], [[style-guide.md]]) as immutable — they were learned, not designed.
- If a session's content demands a new pattern (e.g. a chapter that intercuts two POVs, an NPC POV mid-arc), propose it; don't force the existing rules.
- The Chapter 1 / Korroc-song name-share is **deliberate canon now** even though it started as coincidence. Don't "fix" it.
