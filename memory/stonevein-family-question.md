Korroc and Thane are blood cousins (established in characters/korroc.md and characters/thane.md). Thane's father was Thorek Stonevein, Skyguard of Khar-Zadûn. Whether Korroc *also* carries the Stonevein name is **open** — user said in conversation: "We haven't figured out if we even share the same last name though, the family could have been through a father on one side and mother with the other cousin, whatever the dwarven tradition is for passing down last names."

**The doubled-father canon strengthens the question.** As of the user's character-file expansion, both fathers fought in the Fourth Crusade, both died in it (likely fighting together), and **both dreamed of Khar-Zadûn.** The cousins are "the surviving sons of a doubled grief" and share inheritance of the same broken oath. This significantly raises the stakes on the surname decision — if shared, the Stonevein name is no longer one family's identity but a *lineage's promise.*

**Why:** This matters for prose and for Thane's late-campaign Khar-Zadûn arc. If both cousins are Stonevein, the surname becomes mythically loaded — the Stonelord [[korroc-stonelord]] cousin and the Skyguard-son cousin are *both* expressions of stone-in-blood, and Khar-Zadûn's eventual reclamation is a Stonevein homecoming for the whole line, not just Thane's personal grief.

**How to apply:**
- Do not name Korroc with a surname in prose until the user decides.
- If/when the user picks "shared Stonevein," lean into the literal stone-in-blood thread — it gives the Mythic register depth for both characters.
- If/when the user picks "different surnames through different parents," treat the cousin-bond as still complete but don't assume shared Skyguard legacy.
