# Nageru

## Class & Build
**Archon-Blooded Aasimar** (the **Lawbringer** variant) Monk (**Scaled Fist** archetype) / Paladin (**Oath of Vengeance**). Silent holy warrior. No armor. Fights primarily with fists.

**History: "Marked by the Gods."** His birth was considered a minor miracle by his order — a celestial beacon in a world increasingly shadowed by demonic corruption. The mark on him is not a visible sigil but the *fact* of him: that he was born at all, where he was, when he was. His order saw it; he carries it.

## Appearance
**Six feet, two inches.** Lean yet powerful physique, honed by years of monastic training and martial discipline.

**Skin: warm, sun-kissed bronze.** *Not golden.* (This has been a persistent error in image prompts and in some prior prose — correct it on sight. His Aasimar heritage shows as a subtle aura, not as skin tone.)

**Eyes: striking, intelligent amber**, holding a deep, ancient wisdom.

**A subtle, almost imperceptible golden glow** often emanates from his presence — the actual hallmark of his Aasimar heritage. It is *aura*, not pigment.

**Short, dark brown hair**, kept meticulously neat, often tied back with a simple leather thong.

**No facial scars** — a testament to his disciplined parrying. Hands strong, with the slight calluses of countless hours of practice. He carries himself with **almost preternatural grace** — fluid, economical, never wasted motion.

**Clothing:** simple white monastic wraps — weatherworn, blood-stained more often than not by this point. **Barefoot.** Wears Horgus's old prayer-beads at his wrist (given during the Interlude, Ch 8.5). Carries a few javelins for ranged work; otherwise his only weapons are his fists.

## Voice
- Quiet, formal, occasionally cryptic
- Speaks **deliberately, choosing each word with care** — the careful diction of monastic training
- **Actions often convey more than his speech**
- Uses titles: "brother," "friend"
- Calls everyone formally at first; warms to first names over time
- Slow to speak; means every word
- Capable of dry humor in a deadpan register
- Never raises his voice

## Interior Voice for POV Chapters
When Nageru is POV (rare by design — should land hard when it does), the prose should think in **still-water, breath, and ancestral memory**. He sees the world through the discipline of his order — **Irori's discipline.** He notices stillness, breath, the small motions that betray a creature's intent before it strikes. He carries the memory of his celestial ancestors as a *weight* — not voices, but the sense that **Archon blood** is paying attention. Lawful, watchful, patient.

Examples:
- *"My blood is older than its venom."*
- *"The thunder is patient. The thunder will wait."*
- *"There are no strangers, only kin not yet recognized."*

**Lawbringer layer.** Underneath the stillness runs a steel cord: **the conviction that order is owed to the innocent.** Every meditation is a prayer in motion; every strike is the *restoration of balance* rather than the delivery of punishment. When prose calls for it, surface this — the still water has *weight* underneath, and the weight is law.

His prose should be **sparer than the others'.** Shorter sentences. More silence between paragraphs. He thinks in negative space.

## Background — The Sunken Fist

Nageru was born in the **monastery-fortress of the Sunken Fist** — a secluded bastion of **Irori worshippers** nestled in the sparsely populated foothills bordering the Worldwound itself. His birth was considered a minor miracle, a celestial beacon in a world increasingly shadowed by demonic corruption.

**Parents.** Both still living. Both still at the Sunken Fist, elderly but content in their service to Irori.
- **Elara** (mother): a skilled scribe; meticulously copies and preserves the monastery's sacred texts.
- **Kaelen** (father): groundskeeper and artisan; crafts and maintains the monastery's many intricate mechanisms.
- **No siblings.** His parents believed one divine blessing was enough for a lifetime.

**Upbringing.** Not playful abandon. **Structured learning and self-mastery from the cradle.** Hours in meditation, controlling his breath and focusing his mind. More hours practicing the Sunken Fist's unique martial art — *each strike and block considered a prayer in motion.*

**The pivotal moment (age 8).** A supply caravan he was traveling with was ambushed by monstrous humanoids — common, this close to the Worldwound. The monastery's guardians fought valiantly. Nageru, watching, **saw innocent lives taken.** In that moment a burning conviction ignited in him: a deep-seated need to **protect those who could not protect themselves**, and to bring order to a chaotic world. This is the seed of his Oath of Vengeance — and of his Lawbringer calling.

**Formal training (age 8+).** After the caravan attack his instruction intensified. He came under the wing of two masters:
- **Master Borin** — gruff but kindhearted human Monk. From him Nageru learned the intricate dance of the unarmed warrior, the mastery of ki, and the discipline of body and mind.
- **Sister Lyra** — stern but insightful Aasimar Paladin. From her Nageru absorbed the tenets of divine justice, the sacred vows, and the unwavering commitment to truth and righteousness.

He honed his combat — yes — but he also studied ancient philosophies, legal precedents, and the art of peaceful resolution. The order **recognized him early as a Lawbringer**: an Aasimar destined for a specific path of upholding divine law.

**Leaving the Sunken Fist (age 20).** Feeling the growing unrest and **whispers of a coming storm from the Worldwound**, Nageru received his masters' blessing and set out to put his training to the ultimate test. He was not sent at random. He was *called.* The storm was already gathering.

**Arrival in Kenabres on Armasse day** is therefore not coincidence. He came toward the storm because the Lawbringer in him **felt** it.

## The Celestial Bloodline — Archon-Blooded
Nageru is an **Archon-Blooded Aasimar** — specifically a **Lawbringer.** His celestial heritage is the orderly, judicial host of Archons (the Lawful Good outsiders of Heaven), not the wilder Angelic line. This is the *why* of so much of him: the patience, the law-shaped justice, the inability to take a chaotic shortcut even when one would be easier.

His blood manifests in:
- **Subtle, almost imperceptible golden glow** (aura — not skin)
- **Amber eyes** carrying ancient wisdom
- **Resistance to certain harms** (poison runs slow through him)
- **Divine smites that flare visibly with celestial fire** along his fists
- **An air of quiet authority** that even Horgus eventually responds to
- **A patience that feels older than him**, because in some sense it is

## Faith
- **Devotee of Irori** — the Master of Masters, god of self-perfection, knowledge, history, inner strength. The only mortal who ascended to godhood. Nageru's faith is the patient, disciplined kind; Irori is *worked toward*, not pleaded with.
- **Paladin (Oath of Vengeance)** — sworn against the demons of the Worldwound and the cultists who feed them. The Oath is the formal expression of the conviction that ignited in him at age 8.
- **Monk (Scaled Fist)** — order of the Sunken Fist. Stillness and breath as discipline; charisma and conviction channeling ki as much as strength does.
- His prayers are usually silent. He bows his head briefly and the prayer is done.
- He **lays on hands** with the same matter-of-fact discipline he uses to throw a punch.

## Combat Style
- **Fists first** — flurry of blows, monk discipline
- **Javelin throwing** for ranged work
- **Tumbling** — slips through enemy lines to attack from behind
- **Scaled Fist mechanics** — charisma/conviction channeling, not just raw strength
- **Holy smite empowerment** — his silver scale gives this a major boost
- **Stunning Fist** and related monk abilities (assumed; verify with GM)
- Has the discipline to **stand and keep fighting when others would fall** — repeatedly noted in chapters 1-3
- **Eastern martial-arts vibe in his fighting style, but never goofy.** Treat his combat like a legendary martial artist's, not a comic-relief monk. *Each strike is a prayer in motion.*

## Silver Scale
- Charge: **Divine Smite empowerment for one fight** — for a duration, his unarmed strikes count as good-aligned, magic, and overcome demonic resistances.
- Used in Chapter 3 against the dretches, and again in Chapter 7 to unmake more of them.

## The Stillness
Nageru's defining trait: he is **the still point.** When the world tilts and the others react, Nageru is the one whose breathing doesn't change. This is not coldness — when he laughs (rare), it's warm. When he saves someone, he does it without performance. He is simply *steady,* in a way that the others (especially Korroc) draw on without naming.

## Things Nageru Doesn't Know (Or Doesn't Say)
- He has not yet *told the others* about the Sunken Fist, Irori, the Lawbringer designation, or his parents. He is private by nature. Reveal this through specific in-the-moment triggers, not exposition.
- Whether his order has been told of his arrival in Kenabres.
- The full scope of what he is meant to do here. He came *toward* the storm, but he does not yet know what shape his role takes in it.
