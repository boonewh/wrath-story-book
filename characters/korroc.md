# Korroc Stonevein

## Class & Build
Dwarf Paladin (**Stonelord** + **Oath of the Mendevian Crusade**) / Life Oracle. Warrior-healer hybrid. The Stonelord's bond to the bones of the world is not metaphor for Korroc — **stone literally runs in his veins.** His earth-magic, stone-flesh resilience, and ancestor-deep connection to the clanhold lineage are part of his body, not just his oath. The Oath of the Mendevian Crusade is his second, sworn vow: to do all in his power to find a way to close the Worldwound.

**Oracle Curse:** *Elemental Imbalance: Fire.* His connection to flame runs hot — and the inverse follows. **He is vulnerable to cold.** Frost bites him harder than it bites his companions. Cold spells, freezing breath, the bone-deep chill of certain undead — all hit him for more than they ought to.

## Appearance
Stout dwarf in dented full plate armor. Iron-banded braided beard. Heavy enameled shield bearing the hammer-and-anvil sigil of Torag. Warhammer at his hip. Beard streaked with soot from the forge, even in the underdark. Carries his armor with the unconscious ease of a man who has worn it for years.

**Age — IMPORTANT:** Korroc is **51 — young for a dwarf**, squarely in his prime (dwarves live 350+ years; 51 is a vigorous young adult, NOT old). **Do NOT write him as an old man.** His weariness, gravitas, and "old smith" steadiness come from war, grief, and years at the forge — **not** from age. Where prose wants the weight of years, lean on the *work* and the *loss*, never on him being "old." *(Chapter 8 used "a man past fifty" / "old dwarf" framing — a human-scale read of his age. Correct going forward; see the planned Ch 8 light-touch fix.)*

## Interior Voice for POV Chapters
When Korroc is POV, the prose should think in **forge-imagery and dwarven faith metaphors**. He sees the world as a smith — flaws in metal, the temper of a blade, the heat of a forge, the patient work of shaping iron. His faith is craftsman's faith: Torag is the great Smith, the world is his anvil, and Korroc's job is to be a good tool in the Smith's hand.

Examples:
- *"He felt the wound. He *felt* it, the way a smith feels a flaw in a blade by running his thumb along the edge."*
- *"His faith is craftsman's faith. The world is the anvil. He is a tool in the Smith's hand."*
- His healing spells: "golden warmth," "wash of grace," "the Smith's mercy."
- His attacks: hammer falls "like a smith's hammer on the anvil."

**Stonelord layer (deepens post-Mythic):** Forge-imagery remains Korroc's core register and should never be replaced. But the Stonelord bloodline adds a *stone-metaphor* layer that should begin to surface in his POV chapters — quietly at first, more openly after the Mythic inflection. The stone in him recognizes stone around him. Ancestor-weight sits in his footfalls. The earth answers him when he prays.

Examples:
- *"The stone in him remembered the stone above, and the stone above was weeping."*
- *"He set his palm against the wall. The wall set its palm against him. They knew each other."*
- *"His blood was a slow river through old rock. His grandfathers' blood. Theirs before that. The forge was not the only fire in him."*

Do not replace the forge — **layer the stone underneath.**

## Background
- Smith by trade. Works at a forge in Kenabres alongside Thane.
- Devoted to Torag. Family faith going back generations.
- **His parents — Borin and Dagna Stonevein (named as of Ch 11) — fought in a previous Mendevian Crusade.** His father dreamed of regaining the lost Dwarven Sky City of **Khar-Zadûn** — the same dream that animated Thorek Stonevein, Thane's father. **Both his parents signed the reconstructed Drezen blueprints letter** seventy years ago, alongside Thorek and Helja (Thane's parents) — the first four names on the page. **Dagna's current status is unknown** (do not mark her alive or dead).
- **His father Borin fell in the Fourth Crusade.** Fighting alongside Thorek Stonevein — **the two were brothers of the Stonevein line**, and they shared the same dream. The Fourth was the disaster that broke a generation of dwarven crusaders. Korroc and Thane are not just cousins — they are **the surviving sons of a doubled grief**, and **both carry the Stonevein name** (resolved as canon in the post–Chapter 8 downtime interlude; their fathers were brothers).
- **Demon-hatred from childhood.** He grew up never understanding why anyone would worship such a foul creature. The question was not moral or theological for him — it was *baffled.* Demons stole. Demons defiled. Demons killed his father. The men who served them were broken in a way Korroc cannot conceive of choosing.
- **Has taken the Oath of the Mendevian Crusade.** A sworn paladin's vow: to do all in his power to find a way to **close the Worldwound.** This is not metaphor and not aspiration — it is an oath, and Korroc keeps his oaths.
- Has consecrated at least one underground shrine of Torag and felt it answer (Chapter 1).
- Armasse was supposed to be a holiday.

## Combat Style
- Front-line tank. Walks into melee, takes hits, stays standing.
- Hammer-and-shield. Two-handed swings when he can manage them.
- Spends spells freely on healing his allies, especially mid-combat.
- Will heal someone next to him while another enemy's weapon is inches from his own face. Selfless to a fault.
- **Wound-sharing is TWO separate mechanics (GM clarification, post-Session 12):** (1) **Life Link** — the Oracle revelation, the persistent "thread" from Ch 8; transfers **5 damage per round** from each linked companion to Korroc. (2) **Shield Other** — a spell cast on one ally, layered on top; **half that ally's damage** flows to Korroc (the Session 12 bridge moment with Nageru: "I'll be taking about half your wounds now, Monk"). **In prose, never name either mechanic** — describe it hazily as Korroc strengthening/thickening the bond. Chapter 12's treatment is the model. For continuity math, use the real numbers. **He can also SEVER the link entirely** when he has nothing left to carry with — done for the first time against the chimera (Ch 13), and it should always read as a costly, telling act: the man who carries everyone letting go to stay standing.
- **Mythic reach-healing (Ch 13):** threw a cure spell across fifteen feet of open air to Nageru, landing as though by touch — one of the nameless things the wardstone's fire left in him. Prose keeps it wondrous, not mechanical.
- **Vulnerable to cold** (Oracle Curse: Elemental Imbalance: Fire). Frost-attacks bite him harder than they should. A cold demon, a winter wight, a freezing spell — these are the threats his plate doesn't quite protect him from. When prose calls for a moment of Korroc *failing* to weather a blow, cold is the kind that does it. **CANON (Ch 13): protective magic against cold does NOT work on him.** Caleth's ward against cold settled over him and "found nothing it could hold" — the imbalance runs deeper than warding. Korroc knows this about himself and declines such wards with a head-shake rather than an explanation.
- **Fire-channeling** — the inverse face of his curse. He can call up a small jet of flame from his fingertips when a hammer is the wrong tool. Used sparingly. Best for creatures whose hides shrug off blunt steel — oozes, slimes, the wrong kind of mold. He used it in Chapter 3 to incinerate the trash-ooze ("Squishy").
- **Stonelord stone-strike** — surfaced for the first time in Chapter 4 (against the half-orc Templar's summoned dretch at Irabeth's house). He pressed his free hand to the floor, reached *through* the wood to the stone beneath, and channeled the weight of Kenabres's deep stone through the next hammer-strike. The dretch caved. The hammer wedged in the floor. He had not known he could do it until it answered. **Costly; reserve for the moment that truly needs it.**
- **Life Oracle fire-elemental form** — surfaced for the first time in Chapter 4 (in the Blackwing Library, with Caleth dead at his feet). His body becomes a being of **golden-white fire**, "the fire of a forge at its cleanest, the fire of the moment a blade comes out true." His armor ghosts within the blaze; his outline blurs; he can still wield his hammer. The form does not burn him or his gear. It **pours life into anyone he touches** — Caleth came back from death under his burning hands. The form **collapses if struck hard** (one critical blow ended it in the library). This is mythic-foreshadowing territory; treat any future use with the appropriate weight. He did not know he could do it until it answered.

## Faith
- Paladin of Torag (**Stonelord** + **Oath of the Mendevian Crusade**).
- Oracle of Life — his healing comes from both his oath and a more ancient connection.
- The Stonelord oath ties him to the bones of the world: earth-magic, stone-flesh, ancestor-deep clanhold lineage. Torag is the Smith — but the *stone Torag works* is in Korroc's veins.
- **The Oath of the Mendevian Crusade** is his sworn paladin's vow to close the Worldwound. Not someday. Not in theory. **He has put his hammer and his bones to it.** Every demon he puts down is the oath being kept one more time.
- Believes deeply in oaths, family, hearth, anvil, the keeping of one's word.
- His relationship with Torag is *active*. When he consecrates a place, when he prays for the dead, when he speaks his god's name — something answers.
