# Caleth Aranor

## Class & Build
Half-elf Paladin of Shelyn (**Virtuous Bravo** + **Oath of the Mendevian Crusade**) / Wizard (**Exploiter Wizard** archetype). Mostly Wizard, but his paladin's oath is active and meaningful. Lawful good but **personal-code driven** in the strongest sense: **logic over emotion.** He uses whatever resources are at hand and sees no issue with his actions so long as they further his goals. His goodness is real but instrumental — *means and ends both matter to him, but the ends are the heavier weight.*

## Appearance
Slim half-elven build. Long dark hair tied back. Wears **simple, sturdy traveling clothes with a crusader tabard over them** — **no armor.** Uses *mage armor* spell when expecting combat. Carries a polished glaive of pale wood with a silver leaf-blade, slung across his back. Has a wrist-sheath rigged for a hidden dagger (his own invention, devised young — mocked during his brief time at the Spire and then quietly approved). Carries a spellbook at his hip. Half-elven features are clearly visible — pointed ears, fine bones, the green eyes that mark his Spireborn lineage.

**He does not display Shelyn's sigil or holy symbol openly.** To a glance, his tabard reads as Iomedaean crusader; his Shelynian paladin oath is held privately. (See Faith section.)

**Hidden under his clothing, across one shoulder blade:** the **Seeker's Spiral** — a spiraling sigil that is not scar and not tattoo, but something deeper. The mark of the Riftwardens. He has carried it since birth.

## Voice
- Formal but never stiff
- Measured complete sentences
- "Saint's mercy" is his go-to mild oath (Shelynian, gentle, never blasphemous)
- Slight half-elven formality in greetings, bows
- Capable of dry humor; restrained
- When frustrated: "Saint's *mercy*."
- Calls Thane "Stonevein" when serious, "dwarf" affectionately when not

## Interior Voice for POV Chapters
When Caleth is POV, the prose should think in **artistic-observational terms** layered with **cold strategic calculation.** He genuinely *sees beauty* — even in broken or grotesque things — because his Shelynian oath demands it. But underneath the painter's eye runs a second register: a constant, quiet running of probabilities, threat-assessments, and resource allocations. He notices iconography, body language, the craft of objects, the lines of a face — *and* he notices where the exits are, who is bleeding fastest, which of the company can absorb one more loss. **Where others charge, he positions. Where others rage, he decides.**

Examples (artistic-observational layer):
- *"The thing in the dark was a spider. A *huge* spider, large as a hunting hound, crouched in the middle of the passage."*
- *"Caleth's eye, trained on iconography from the day his foster parents had first put a brush in his hand, catalogued the gear at a glance."*
- *"His painter's eye drank it. It was beautiful. It was a *home.*"*

Examples (calculating layer — should grow more visible in his POV chapters):
- *"He had three spells left. He had the dagger. He had thirty seconds before the second one came out of the dark. He chose."*
- *"Korroc would absorb the blow. Korroc always absorbed the blow. The question was whether Korroc could absorb the next one, and the one after that — and Caleth had already begun the arithmetic."*
- *"A man who plans for every future cannot undo a single past. He had learned that. He had learned it badly."*

**Two cards held close, always.** He doesn't say everything he's thinking. His interior monologue often notes what he is choosing *not* to say aloud. This is half-elven distance and the Lantern Seer's discipline both — the watcher who has spent his life imitating humanity carefully, and the calculator who knows that a card revealed is a card spent.

**He uses whatever resources are at hand**, and his lawful-good oath does not flinch at hard choices. If saving the company means letting a thing happen that he would not have chosen in a kinder world, he will let it happen and live with it. The Seer trained him to live *ahead*, not in the moment — and a man who lives ahead has already paid for his future choices.

## Background — The Riftwarden's Son

Caleth Aranor was never meant to be ordinary. Even his birth seemed like a riddle half-told and half-forgotten.

**Spireborn by blood, not by upbringing.** Caleth is a **Spireborn half-elf** — the lineage is real and is on his character sheet. But he did not grow up at the Mordant Spire. His parents, Spireborn themselves, fled the Spire when he was very young to join the **Riftwardens** — the secretive guardians who watch where reality thins. He has spent *some* time at the Spire later in life (brief, formative-but-not-defining), but his real childhood was elsewhere. **Do not constantly refer to him as Spireborn in prose.** Treat the lineage as a background fact, not his primary identity.

**The Seeker's Spiral.** Across one shoulder blade is a spiraling sigil — not scar, not tattoo. Something deeper. Something *remembered.* The midwife noticed it the day he was born. His foster parents would later name it in careful tones: the **Seeker's Spiral**, the sign of the **Riftwardens.**

**The Riftwarden parents.** His true mother and father were Riftwardens who **vanished into the Worldwound less than a month after his birth**, on a mission no one would explain. The official word was *lost.* Caleth has never believed that. *Lost* implies chance. His instincts whispered something sharper — someone, or something, ended them, and somewhere beyond the horizon of his life, that ending still breathes.

**Raised in Kenabres.** A human family took him in and raised him with earnest kindness, though they never quite understood him. He grew tall and fine-featured, his elven heritage sharpening his gaze and softening his years, but it was his **distance** that unsettled people. Even as a child, he watched rather than joined; listened rather than spoke. He studied the patterns in other children's games. He learned early to imitate humanity the way one might learn a language: carefully, deliberately, never quite natively.

**Magic came to him like a second pulse.** Cantrips slipped from his fingers before he understood their structure. Books — arcane, obscure, sometimes dangerous — felt less like lessons and more like *reunions.* The techniques of **Exploiter Wizard** arcane-bending came to him as if remembered, not learned.

**The Lantern Seer.** An old figure in Kenabres, known only by that name. Some called them a fraud; others crossed the street to avoid their gaze. Caleth listened. Under the Seer's guidance he began to see flashes himself — futures as branching rivers, some dammed, some poisoned, some rushing toward catastrophe. **Three visions, in particular, organized him:**

- *a city burning under a bleeding sky* — Armasse. Kenabres. Khorramzadeh. **Already arrived.**
- *a blade wreathed in radiance* — **Radiance, Yaniel's sword.** Already recovered. He currently carries it.
- *a shadow that wore a man's shape but cast none of its own* — almost certainly **S. V.** Not yet faced.

The visions did not frighten him. They **organized** him. Where others lived day to day, Caleth began to live *ahead.* Every action became a piece on a board only he could see.

**The Secret That Hunts Him.** There is a name Caleth does not speak. Years ago, before he fully understood the weight of consequence, his caution failed him — through inaction, or cold calculation, or both, he allowed something terrible to happen that cost another person dearly. The details remain buried beneath layers of rationalization and silence, but **the truth gnaws at him in quiet moments.** This is the paradox at his core: a man who plans for every future, yet cannot undo a single past decision. It is also why he holds himself to an unyielding standard now.

**A wanderer of empty connections.** He has known companions and fleeting romances; none have endured. There is always a distance he cannot close, a part of himself already standing in some future moment, measuring outcomes. People sense it. They drift away. He tells himself this is acceptable — attachments complicate plans. And yet, when he watches others laugh unburdened by tomorrow, **something in him goes quiet.**

**Why the Worldwound is personal.** The mark on his back burns more often now. His visions sharpen. The Worldwound calls — not as a place, but as **an answer.** Somewhere within that scar upon reality lies the truth of his parents' fate. Somewhere, the one responsible still walks. Caleth Aranor will be the blade that severs the coming darkness, or the mind that fails to stop it. He intends, with ruthless certainty, that it will be the former.

## Campaign Trait: Riftwarden Orphan

**Status: Secret from the party.** None of the others know about the Riftwardens, the Spiral, or his parents. He has not told them. He may never tell them unprompted. The trait is plot-relevant and should drive his choices around portal-magic, planar lore, and anyone who recognizes the sign.

## Faith — Shelyn
Caleth is a Paladin of **Shelyn** (goddess of love, beauty, and art), with two stacked archetypes: **Virtuous Bravo** + **Oath of the Mendevian Crusade.** His paladin's oath is **mercy-shaped**, not justice-shaped. He believes:
- That beauty has teeth and a love-song hides a sword in its sheath
- That mercy cuts truer than spite
- That the broken thing can still be the holy thing
- That every face deserves to be seen

**Quietly reserved about his faith.** Caleth does **not** display Shelyn's sigil or holy symbol openly. To a glance, his crusader tabard reads Iomedaean; his Shelynian oath is held privately. *"Saint's mercy"* is the most public his faith gets. This is partly Spireborn habit (hold the cards close), partly tactical (a Shelynian half-elf paladin-wizard is a *very* identifying combination), and partly genuine humility — he believes beauty announces itself, and the believer should not.

**Virtuous Bravo** shapes how the oath expresses in combat: he does not embody the thunderous, unbending paladin many expect. Instead he is **precise. Calculated.** His courage is not loud but *exacting* — perfect timing, deliberate strikes, unwavering control. To some this makes him seem distant, even cold. To others, terrifyingly reliable.

**Oath of the Mendevian Crusade** is sworn: to do all in his power to close the Worldwound. For Caleth, this is also personal — the Worldwound is where his parents are.

This puts him in interesting tension with the more hammer-driven theology of Korroc's Torag-worship. The two of them are working out something together, paladin to paladin.

## Combat Style
- **Virtuous Bravo positioning** — he does not charge. He angles, withdraws, re-enters at the seam. Every strike is decided before it lands.
- **Glaive at reach** — leaf-blade thrusts from behind the front line
- **Hidden dagger** at the wrist for emergencies
- **Wizard spells:** mage armor, shocking grasp, dimensional step, snowball, light, and others
- **Smite + Shocking Grasp combo** — his signature move, blending divine and arcane into a touch attack
- **Lay on hands** — used freely on allies in need
- **Exploiter Wizard arcane-bending** — he can twist magical theory with instinct and will, treating spells as components to be reassembled rather than recipes to be followed
- Will throw daggers when forced (his throwing accuracy is "deadly accurate" per the original brief)
- **Has surprises.** His character sheet hints at hidden capabilities not yet visible to the company; lean into the unexpected when the moment calls for it.

## The Dragon's Secret
**This is critical and private:**

Caleth stood three feet from Terendelev in the crowd at Armasse, before her transformation. She was disguised as a plain human woman in grey traveler's clothes. When the wardstone fell and she revealed her true form, she **made direct eye contact with Caleth** and gave him a small, apologetic, "here we go" smile in the half-second before launching to fight Khorramzadeh.

He believes she **recognized him** as a Spireborn paladin of Shelyn, and that her last word — the silver scale's gift — was given to him deliberately. His scale is the only one he hasn't spent. He believes it is for something specific.

In Chapter 2, he dreamed of her on a cliff above a sea, and she said:
> *"There is one of yours coming who will need you to be the kind one. Be the kind one, Spireborn. Even when the others choose the hammer. *Be the kind one.*"*

He has not told the others any of this.
